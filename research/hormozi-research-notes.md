## 1. Foundational Definitions

### What is a lead?

> "A lead is someone you can reach out to."
> - paraphrase, $100M Leads (cross-cited by [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/), [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads))

A lead is just contact information. It is NOT a customer, not a prospect, not pre-qualified.

### What is an engaged lead?

An engaged lead has actively signaled interest: clicked an ad, replied to an email, consumed your content, booked a call. The distinction matters because Hormozi's framework optimizes for engaged leads, not raw contact volume.

> "Engaged lead: someone who knows you, likes you, and trusts you - they've consumed your content, benefited from your free offerings, and developed a relationship."
> - paraphrased from [Loxie](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads)

### The lead-gen equation

> More Stuff x Better Stuff = More Leads
> - paraphrased from [Loxie](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads)

Two levers only: volume (More) and quality (Better). The "New" axis (new channels) is the third multiplier when the first two plateau.

## 2. The Value Equation (governing principle)

This is the load-bearing framework. Every offer, lead magnet, hook, ad, and email must be auditable against it.

> Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
> - $100M Offers, cited by [Medium / Joe McMahan](https://medium.com/@joemcmahan/the-value-equation-436ad5fe5a3a), [Creator Economy](https://creatoreconomy.so/p/the-value-equation-for-irresistible-products), [PowerMoves](https://thepowermoves.com/100-million-offers-summary-review/)

### The four levers

1. **Dream Outcome** - what the customer wants. Sell the destination, not the journey. Maximize.
2. **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** - do they believe it will work for them? Built via proof, guarantees, specificity, and case studies. Maximize.
3. **Time Delay** - how long until they get the result? Make it feel instant. Minimize.
4. **Effort & Sacrifice** - how hard is it for them? Minimize. The bottom of the equation is where most offers die.

### Hormozi's emphasis (cross-cited)

Most operators over-invest in the numerator (bigger dream, more proof) and under-invest in the denominator. The denominator is the cheat code: speed and ease.

### Field test (use this checklist on every output)

For any artifact (offer, hook, lead magnet, sequence), score 1-10 on each lever:
- Dream outcome: is it named in the prospect's own language? Score: __ / 10
- Perceived likelihood: what proof is on the page? Score: __ / 10
- Time delay: when do they get the first win? Score: __ / 10
- Effort & sacrifice: what did you remove from their plate? Score: __ / 10

Total / 40. Below 28 = fail. Below 32 = ship only if time-boxed test.

## 3. The 4-Step Lead-Gen Process

The book maps the lead-gen problem onto four sequential questions. Every operator must have an answer to all four before running a campaign.

| Step | Question | What it produces |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Who do I sell to? | ICP definition |
| 2 | What do I offer them? | Grand Slam Offer (see Section 6) |
| 3 | How do I get them to know I exist? | Core Four channels (see Section 4) |
| 4 | How do I make them want it? | Lead Magnet + hooks + follow-up (see Sections 5, 7, 9) |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) cross-validated against [Loxie](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads), [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads). General consensus.

### Diagnostic question for any failing campaign

Walk the four steps in order. The first one with a vague answer is the constraint. Fixing step 4 when step 1 is broken is wasted motion.

## 4. The Core Four Channels (with activation order)

> "There are only four ways to advertise. Two audience types (warm or cold) crossed with two communication styles (one-to-one or one-to-many)."
> - paraphrased from $100M Leads, cross-cited by [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/), [Greg Faxon](https://www.gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-leads-summary), [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/).

| | One-to-One | One-to-Many |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm audience** | Warm Outreach | Posting Free Content |
| **Cold audience** | Cold Outreach | Paid Ads |

### Activation order (Hormozi's prescribed sequence)

1. **Warm Outreach** - first, because it has the highest reply rate and zero cost. You already have an audience; mine it before buying one.
2. **Posting Free Content** - second, because it compounds. Audience is the asset, not the content.
3. **Cold Outreach** - third, because it scales beyond your warm list but requires Big Fast Value.
4. **Paid Ads** - fourth, because it's the most expensive and least forgiving. Don't run paid until your offer converts on free channels.

> Source: [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads) explicit activation order. Cross-validated against [Loxie](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads).

### 4.1 Warm Outreach (10-step process)

From [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/), cross-validated against [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/):

1. Get your list (every contact: phone, email, social, alumni, ex-colleagues).
2. Pick a platform (one at a time; don't fragment).
3. Personalize your message (reference something specific, not "hope you're well").
4. Reach out to 100 people daily.
5. Act like a human - use the **ACA framework**:
   - **Acknowledge** what they said.
   - **Compliment** them (link to a positive trait).
   - **Ask** another question that nudges toward your offer.
6. Make them an offer.
7. Make it easy - offer free service initially to break the ice.
8. Move to the next platform when the first is exhausted.
9. Start charging - gradually raise prices once you have proof.
10. Keep the list warm - provide ongoing value to dormant contacts.

**Benchmarks (warm outreach):**
- 20% engagement rate (replies)
- 20% of replies accept the free offer
- 25% of free-offer takers convert to paid

Result: **1 paying customer per 100 reach-outs**.

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "Benchmarks: How Well Am I Doing?" section.

### 4.2 Posting Free Content (Hook-Retain-Reward)

The content unit has three components, each measured by a different metric.

| Component | Job | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Grab initial attention | Consumption start rate (3-second-views, opens, scroll-stops) |
| **Retain** | Sustain attention through the body | Watch time, completion rate, scroll depth |
| **Reward** | Deliver promised value | Shares, saves, return visits, comments |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "The Content Unit: Three Core Components." Cross-validated against [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads).

#### Retention methods (3 named)

- **Lists** - sequential numbered items.
- **Steps** - ordered actions toward a specific outcome.
- **Stories** - narratives with embedded lessons.

#### Hook source categories (5 named)

- Far Past - historical lessons.
- Recent Past - client stories, last week's meeting.
- Present - current observations.
- Trending - applying current events to your domain.
- Manufactured - stunts, experiments you set up to write about.

#### Headline elements (7 named)

> "Ongoing, Unusual, Conflict, Proximity, Celebrity, Recency, Relevancy"
> - [On the Agile Path](https://digital-garden.ontheagilepath.net/comprehensive-summary-based-on-my-highlights)

Use one or more in every headline.

#### Give-to-Ask Ratio

> "Approximately 3.5:1"
> - [On the Agile Path](https://digital-garden.ontheagilepath.net/comprehensive-summary-based-on-my-highlights), [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/)

Established platforms can afford 3.5 give for every 1 ask. New platforms should "give until they ask" - provide 10x value before any monetization.

### 4.3 Cold Outreach (3-problem solution)

Cold outreach faces three structural problems. Each has a specific solution.

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| No contact info | Build a targeted list (scrape, buy, intent data) |
| Messages ignored | **Personalize + Big Fast Value** |
| Lack of interest | Volume + persistence (multi-touch, multi-channel) |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "How Cold Outreach Works."

#### The Big Fast Value formula (cold)

The opening of every cold message should deliver something useful BEFORE asking for anything. Examples (from the third-party reconstructions, since the official PDFs were inaccessible):

- A free audit of one specific thing on their site.
- A specific observation: "I noticed your checkout flow loses people at step 3 - here's the 4-line fix."
- A relevant case study with a numeric outcome from a similar business.

> The exact Hormozi cold script PDF could not be text-extracted (binary PDF). Reconstructions from [School of SDR](https://schoolofsdr.substack.com/p/100m-cold-email-strategy-by-alex), [Money Tent](https://medium.com/@moneytent/alex-hormozis-100m-cold-email-strategy-fc150bdae085), [Opps.ai](https://www.opps.ai/blog-posts/alex-hormozis-100-million-cold-email-strategy) converge on: personalize, give big fast value, soft CTA, follow up across channels.

#### Cold outreach structure (synthesized from cross-source reconstruction)

```
Opening (1 line): Specific observation about THEM, not you.
Big Fast Value (1-3 sentences): The thing they can take and use.
Soft CTA (1 line): Low-friction yes/no question OR offer to send more.
Sign-off: Name only, no signature block, no logo, no hype.
```

#### Follow-up cadence

> "It takes 8 touchpoints to convert"
> - [School of SDR](https://schoolofsdr.substack.com/p/100m-cold-email-strategy-by-alex) reconstruction

Hormozi explicitly says: if no response after the first message, follow up **at least 2-3 times** before moving on.

> Source: [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/) "Follow-Up Rules."

Cross-channel switching is encouraged: email -> LinkedIn -> phone -> email. The same prospect across multiple channels increases reply rate without increasing perceived spam.

### 4.4 Paid Ads (What-Who-When + 3-Phase Scaling)

#### Ad copy structure: What-Who-When

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "Step #3: 'But what should my ad say?' -> Call Out + Value + Call to Action."

- **What** - the dream outcome and its nightmare opposite. "Get X without Y."
- **Who** - how the product changes how others see them (status).
- **When** - past consequences (regret), present pain, future risk (urgency).

#### 3-Phase Paid Ad Scaling

| Phase | Goal | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Track Money | Set up measurement | Don't scale before you can attribute. |
| 2. Lose Money | Find profitable creative | Budget = 2x your 30-day profit. Expect to lose. |
| 3. Print Money | Scale winners aggressively | Compound the few ads that beat the CAC threshold. |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "How much do I spend?: The Three Phases of Scaling Paid Ads."

#### Client-Financed Acquisition (the unit-economics rule)

> First-30-day Gross Profit (including upsells) >= Customer Acquisition Cost.

If this holds, every customer you acquire pays for the next one within 30 days. Cash-flow infinite. If it doesn't hold, paid ads will eat your business.

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/), [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads).

#### LTGP:CAC ratio

Healthy floor: **3:1** (Lifetime Gross Profit to Customer Acquisition Cost).

> Source: [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads) "CAC-to-LTGP healthy ratio."

## 5. Lead Magnets

### Definition

> "A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem that, once solved, reveals another problem solved by your core offer."
> - paraphrased from $100M Leads, cited by [VidTao](https://blog.vidtao.com/alex-hormozi-lead-magnet-framework/)

The "salty pretzel" metaphor: a bar gives free salty pretzels; customers get thirsty; they buy drinks. A lead magnet must create thirst that only your paid offer can quench.

### The 3 lead magnet types

| Type | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Reveal Problem** (Diagnosis) | Surfaces a problem the prospect didn't know they had | Free audit, assessment, scorecard |
| **Sample / Trial** | Limited-scope or limited-time access to the core offer | 14-day trial, one free session |
| **One Step in Multi-Step Process** | Solves one valuable step; the rest is paid | "Email subject-line generator" when the paid product is a full email course |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "Step 2: Three Types of Lead Magnets," cross-validated against [VidTao](https://blog.vidtao.com/alex-hormozi-lead-magnet-framework/).

### The 4 delivery methods

| Method | Examples |
|---|---|
| Software | Calculators, spreadsheets, dashboards, mini-apps |
| Information | PDFs, courses, video, guides, interviews |
| Services | Audits, consultations, free first session |
| Physical | Books, samples, templates printed |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "Step 3: Four Delivery Methods."

### The 7-step lead magnet creation process

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "Seven Steps To Creating an Effective Lead Magnet," cross-validated against [VidTao](https://blog.vidtao.com/alex-hormozi-lead-magnet-framework/) and [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads).

1. **Figure out the problem** - narrow AND meaningful. Pick one.
2. **Figure out how to solve it** - one of the 3 types above.
3. **Figure out how to deliver it** - one of the 4 methods.
4. **Test the name** - name it as the solution to the problem, not the format. "5-Minute Mortgage Calculator," not "Free Spreadsheet."
5. **Make it easy to consume** - under 5 minutes ideally. No setup friction.
6. **Make it darn good** - overdeliver to the point that it feels weird it's free.
7. **Make it easy for them to ask for more** - clear CTA to the paid offer.

### Hormozi's "feels obligated to pay" test

> "If your lead magnet is so valuable that people feel obligated to pay you, you've done it right."
> - paraphrased, [Greg Faxon](https://www.gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-leads-summary)

If a recipient could imagine paying for it, the magnet is doing its job.

## 6. The Grand Slam Offer (from $100M Offers)

The lead magnet pulls them in; the Grand Slam Offer closes them.

### Core formula

A Grand Slam Offer combines:
1. **Unmatched value** (Value Equation maxed)
2. **Clear uniqueness** (incomparable to alternatives)
3. **Risk reversal** (guarantees)
4. **Tiered pricing** (anchor high, sell middle)
5. **Bonus stack** (each item named and priced separately)
6. **Time-bound scarcity / urgency**

> Source: [PowerMoves](https://thepowermoves.com/100-million-offers-summary-review/), [Greg Faxon (Offers)](https://www.gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-offers-summary).

### 7 steps to a Grand Slam Offer

1. Identify Dream Outcome
2. List Problems (every obstacle and limiting belief)
3. Solutions List (turn each problem into a "how to")
4. Create Delivery Vehicles
5a. Trim & Stack - drop low-value, keep high-value/low-cost
5b. Bundle Together - package into one offer

> Source: [PowerMoves](https://thepowermoves.com/100-million-offers-summary-review/) "7 Steps To Grand Slam Offers."

### Scarcity types (3)

- Limited supply (only N seats / N spots)
- Limited time (deadline)
- Limited bonuses (bonus disappears)

### Urgency types (4)

- Rolling deadlines (cohort-based, recurring)
- Seasonal windows (Q4 only, year-end)
- Pricing increases (price goes up on date X)
- Exploding opportunity (one-time chance)

### Guarantee types (4)

- **Unconditional** - no questions asked, money back.
- **Conditional** - money back IF you complete the program / do the work.
- **Anti-guarantee** - final sale, no refunds (used to filter for committed buyers).
- **Implied** - performance-based ("only pay when you win").

### MAGIC naming formula

- **M**agnet - compelling reason
- **A**vatar - target customer
- **G**oal - desired outcome
- **I**nterval - time frame
- **C**ontainer - wrapper word (system, blueprint, formula, machine)

> Source: [PowerMoves](https://thepowermoves.com/100-million-offers-summary-review/), [Greg Faxon (Offers)](https://www.gregfaxon.com/blog/100m-offers-summary).

Example: "30-Day Founder Cash-Flow Blueprint" -> avatar (founder), goal (cash-flow), interval (30-day), container (blueprint), magnet (the implicit promise).

## 7. Hooks and CTAs

### Hook rule

> "The hook - the first thing someone sees - determines roughly 80% of an ad's performance."
> - paraphrased, [Loxie](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads)

### Hook types (synthesized from sources)

The book does not give a closed taxonomy of hook types. Cross-source reconstruction yields:

- **Curiosity** - open a loop ("The 3-word phrase that closed our biggest deal")
- **Contrarian** - invert conventional wisdom ("Stop sending follow-up emails")
- **Status** - what the audience wants to be seen as ("How serious founders handle Q4")
- **Proof** - specific number ("$47k in 11 days from one cold campaign")
- **Specificity** - narrow detail ("The 4-line tweak that doubled our checkout completion")

> General consensus, unsourced as a closed list. The mechanic is verifiable; the labels are field-standard.

### CTA rule

A CTA must be:
- Specific (one action)
- Low-friction (one click, one reply, one form field)
- Time-bound when possible (urgency)

## 8. More-Better-New (scaling framework)

When you've launched, scale on three axes in this order:

| Axis | What it means | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **More** | Increase volume of current actions | First. Run the Rule of 100. |
| **Better** | Improve efficiency of current actions | Second. One test per week per platform. |
| **New** | Add new platforms / channels | Third. Only after More and Better plateau. |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "How To Get Even More Leads: More Better New," cross-validated against [Loxie](https://loxie.app/learnwithloxie/100m-leads), [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads).

### The Rule of 100

100 daily marketing actions for 100 consecutive days. Per Core Four channel:

- **Warm**: 100 reach-outs/day
- **Content**: 100 minutes/day creating (minimum 1 piece shipped)
- **Cold**: 100 reach-outs/day (often automated)
- **Paid**: 100 minutes/day on ad creative + committed daily budget

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "More: The Power of Volume."

The Rule of 100 is non-negotiable in the book. You don't get to scale Better until you've maxed More.

## 9. Follow-Up Rules and Cadence

### Hormozi's explicit rule

> "If a lead doesn't respond, follow up at least 2-3 times. If still no response, move forward."
> - paraphrased, [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/)

### Cross-source synthesis

- **Touchpoint count**: minimum 8 (cross-channel)
- **Channel switching**: yes - same person across email, LinkedIn, phone is fine and increases reply rate
- **Add value every touch**: never just "bumping this up." Every follow-up should add a new piece of value or a new angle.

> Source: [School of SDR](https://schoolofsdr.substack.com/p/100m-cold-email-strategy-by-alex) reconstruction.

### Reactivation (warm list maintenance)

> "Keep your list warm - provide ongoing value to dormant contacts."
> - paraphrased, [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/)

The warm list is an asset. It decays without maintenance. Quarterly value drops to dormant contacts is the minimum.

## 10. List-Building Principles

The book treats list-building as a downstream output of the Core Four, not a separate discipline. Sources:

| Channel | List-building mechanic |
|---|---|
| Warm | Compile every contact you've ever had. The list already exists; you've just never extracted it. |
| Content | Followers + email signups via lead magnet. Your content makes the list grow on autopilot. |
| Cold | Targeted scrape / purchase / intent data. The list is bought or built, not earned. |
| Paid | Lookalike audiences seeded from your existing list. The list is the input, not the output. |

> Source: [Shortform](https://www.shortform.com/blog/100m-leads-alex-hormozi/) "List-Building."

### The compounding loop

```
Warm list -> seeds Content audience -> seeds Lookalike for Paid
Content audience -> generates Cold targets (people who engaged but never replied)
Cold replies -> become Warm list
Paid conversions -> become Warm list
```

Every channel feeds every other channel. The list is the closed-loop asset.

## 11. Lead Getters (4 leverage mechanisms)

When you've maxed your own time, four entities can generate leads for you:

| Lead Getter | Mechanic | Hormozi system |
|---|---|---|
| **Customer referrals** | Word of mouth via goodwill | Bake referral asks into delivery moments |
| **Employees** | Replicated process | **Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate (3D)** |
| **Agencies** | Pay for mentorship, then outperform | Hire as teachers, fire when you've absorbed |
| **Affiliates** | Tiered commissions for promoting your offer | Tiered structure to scale exponentially |

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "Outline of The 'Lead Getters' Section."

### The Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate (3D) employee training model

1. **Document** - perform the task, write a checklist, record video while doing it.
2. **Demonstrate** - perform the task again with the employee watching, walking through the checklist.
3. **Duplicate** - employee performs the task, you watch, refine until consistent.

> Source: [HowToes](https://howtoes.blog/2025/06/02/100m-leads-book-summary/) "How To Get Employees To Get You Leads."

## 12. Anti-Patterns (things Hormozi explicitly rejects)

Codify these as hard nos in the skill:

1. **Generic openings.** "Hope this finds you well." Hormozi rejects this. Personalize or don't send.
2. **Asking before giving.** Cold messages that lead with the ask. Always lead with Big Fast Value.
3. **Over-relying on one channel.** Single-channel businesses are fragile. The Core Four is a portfolio, not a menu.
4. **Polishing before posting.** "Better is the enemy of done" applies - Rule of 100 first, optimization second.
5. **Multi-problem lead magnets.** A lead magnet that solves three problems poorly converts worse than one that solves one problem completely.
6. **Naming the format, not the outcome.** "Free PDF" is dead. "5-Minute X Calculator" is alive.
7. **One-touch follow-up.** Single-message campaigns leave 80%+ of conversions on the table.
8. **Scaling Paid before product converts.** Paid ads will accelerate a broken funnel into bankruptcy.
9. **Mock customer talk.** ChatGPT-generated personalization is detectable and counter-productive. Real research or no research.
10. **Skipping the unit economics check.** No campaign launches without LTGP/CAC math. Period.

## 13. The 7 Levels of Business Growth (closing context)

Hormozi maps growth across 7 levels. This is operator framing, useful for ICP alignment:

1. Friends and family
2. Known audiences (referral)
3. Hiring (delegated lead gen)
4. Referrals (systematized)
5. Multi-platform (Core Four scaled)
6. Executives (agency / leadership layer)
7. $100M+ autonomy

> Source: [Insight Books](https://insightbooks.app/books/100m-leads) "Idea 6: Building the $100M Machine."

For Catalyst Works / Signal Works (current state, sub-$10k MRR), Levels 1-3 are the realistic operating range. Level 4 (referrals as a system) is the next milestone. Levels 5+ are far out and shouldn't shape Phase 5 templates.

## Message Template A: Warm Reach-Out (1st touch)

**Use when:** Past colleague, alumni, mutual connection, past vendor. Anyone you have a real prior relationship with, regardless of how long ago.

**Channel:** Whatever you used last. LinkedIn DM if that was the medium, email if that was, phone if that was. Match the prior channel; don't switch unprovoked.

**Length:** ~80 words. No more.

```
Hi [First name],

[Personalization hook, one specific detail. Not "hope you're well." Something real. Examples:
  - "Saw the photo of [their kid] on LinkedIn. Last time I saw [them] was at [event]."
  - "Still think about that conversation we had at [GE / project / event] about [specific topic]."
  - "Caught your post about [thing]. The point about [specific detail] landed."]

Quick reason I'm reaching out: I started Catalyst Works last year. We diagnose the operational bottleneck slowing services firms down. Most of what we find: one approval loop, one handoff, one pricing decision that costs 6-8% margin nobody is looking at.

I'm building a small group of people I trust to send this to before it's polished. You're one of them.

If you know anyone running a services firm who'd benefit from a 90-minute diagnostic, I'd love an intro. If not, no worries, and I'd love to catch up regardless.

Boubacar
```

**Why this structure (per Hormozi 10-step warm):**
- Step 3 (personalize): the personalization hook is REAL, not "hope you're well."
- Step 5 (act like a human): the "no worries, and I'd love to catch up regardless" closes the ACA loop without pressure.
- Step 6 (make them an offer): the offer is the diagnostic, framed as a referral ask. This is Hormozi's free-offer + ACA combined.
- Step 7 (make it easy): one ask, one alternative ("intro OR catch up"). Either is a yes.
- No "circle back" / "leverage" / sales sludge.

**Send rate target:** 10-25 per day for 4-10 days until 100 are sent. (Rule of 100, distributed.)

## Message Template B: Warm Reactivation (quarterly value drop)

**Use when:** Contact is dormant (> 6 months no interaction) AND they did not respond to Template A.

**Channel:** Email by default (lower-pressure than DM). If never had email, use the original channel.

**Length:** ~60 words. No ask.

```
Subject: One thing I noticed

Hi [First name],

Not following up on anything. Just sharing something I learned this quarter that you'd find useful given [specific context: their company size / industry / role].

[The thing, one paragraph max. Examples:
  - "The 4-line robots.txt fix that opens up AI-tool visibility for any local business."
  - "The single approval-loop pattern that costs services firms 6-8% margin in Q4."
  - "Why GE's tier-2 supplier audit produces 10x more savings than the tier-1 audit it's modeled on."]

If useful, you'll know what to do with it. If not, no harm done.

Boubacar
```

**Why this structure (per Hormozi):**
- "Keep your list warm" rule (Step 10 of warm process).
- Pure value drop. No ask. Hormozi's give-to-ask ratio at its strongest (infinite).
- 100% of the work is in the value content. The wrapper is intentionally bland.

**Send rate target:** Once per quarter to every dormant contact. Date these in the sheet so you don't double-send.

## ACA Reply Framework (when they respond)

Hormozi's "act like a human" mechanic. Use on every warm reply.

| Step | What to do | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **A**cknowledge | Restate what they said. Show you read it. | "That makes sense, you're already 6 months into the GTM rebuild." |
| **C**ompliment | Praise something real. Link to a positive trait. | "Most operators put off that work for years. You went straight at it." |
| **A**sk | One question that nudges toward your offer (or toward continuing the conversation). | "Out of curiosity, what's the bottleneck that's been hardest to name in that rebuild?" |

**Anti-pattern:** never use ACA on every single reply blindly. If they say "yes, intro me to person X," respond with the intro, not with ACA. The framework is for ambiguous replies, not closed ones.

## Process Steps (the 10-step warm sequence as a checklist)

Run this as a Boubacar-personal motion. Tools optional. Discipline required.

- [ ] **Day 0:** Build the 100-name list. Stop at 100. (Rule: list-building is not lead-gen. Deadline: end of day.)
- [ ] **Days 1-7:** Send Template A to 15 names per day. Personalize each one. No automation.
- [ ] **Days 1-14:** Reply to every response within 24 hours using ACA.
- [ ] **Day 14:** Tally results. Hormozi benchmark: 20% engagement (replies). 1 in 4 of those takes the free offer. 1 in 4 of those converts.
- [ ] **Day 30:** Re-engage anyone who replied but didn't book. One light bump with new value.
- [ ] **Day 90:** Send Template B to anyone who never replied to Template A.
- [ ] **Quarterly forever:** Template B to all dormant contacts.

## Success Criteria (explicit floors)

Hormozi's published warm-outreach benchmarks:

| Metric | Target | Floor (don't go below) |
|---|---|---|
| Reply rate | 20% of sends | 10% over 100 sends |
| Free-offer accept rate | 25% of repliers | 15% |
| Free-to-paid conversion | 25% of free-offer takers | 10% |
| Net: paying customer per 100 reach-outs | 1.25 | 0.5 |

**If actuals fall below the floor over a 100-name batch:** the constraint is in the list quality (not enough HIGHEST-priority names) or the personalization (too generic), not the template. Re-build the list before re-sending.

## Anti-Patterns Refused

- Bulk sending the same message to all 100. (Not warm anymore.)
- Mailmerge with `{first_name}` only. (Not personalized.)
- Sending to anyone you've never met. (That's cold; use the cold playbook.)
- Sending to current paying clients. (They go through engagement-ops, not lead-gen.)
- Mass-sending Template B without prior Template A attempt. (Reactivation requires prior contact.)

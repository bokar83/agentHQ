## How to Use

This file consolidates objection responses for both CW and SW discovery calls. It's NOT a script - it's a pattern bank.

**The frame:** every objection has two layers - the surface objection (the words) and the underlying Value Equation lever (the unspoken concern). Address the lever, not the words.

| Surface objection | Underlying VE lever |
|---|---|
| "It's too expensive" | Perceived Likelihood weak (will it work for me?) |
| "I need to think about it" | Time Delay or Effort feels too high |
| "How do I know this works?" | Perceived Likelihood weak |
| "I don't have time" | Effort & Sacrifice perceived as high |
| "We already have someone who does that" | Dream Outcome unclear (why switch?) |
| "Send me more info" | All four levers under-specified; they don't have a frame to evaluate |

When you hear an objection, name the lever silently to yourself. Then respond to that lever, not the words.

## Catalyst Works Objections

### "It's too expensive ($497)"

**Lever:** Perceived Likelihood.

**Response:** "Fair. Here's the math: most operators I've worked with find one bottleneck pattern in the 90 minutes that's costing them 3-5 points of margin. On a $5M services firm, that's $150-250K a year. The session pays for itself if we find one real pattern. If we don't, you don't owe me anything for the next 30 minutes - I keep my standards tight on that."

**Why this works:**
- Reframes the cost in the prospect's revenue terms.
- Adds conditional risk reversal (Hormozi guarantee type).
- Doesn't apologize for the price.

---

### "I need to think about it"

**Lever:** Time Delay or Effort.

**Response:** ACA + offer reduction.
- **A:** "Totally fair - you don't want to commit to something without sitting with it."
- **C:** "Most operators who hesitate here are the ones who eventually get the most out of the session, because they take it seriously."
- **A:** "What's the biggest thing you'd want to know between now and a yes?"

**Anti-pattern:** "What's holding you back?" Hormozi rejects. Sounds aggressive.

**Fallback if they still won't book:** "I'll send the calendar link with one quote held for 7 days. If you want it, click. If not, no follow-up from me on this."

---

### "We already have a consultant / advisor"

**Lever:** Dream Outcome unclear.

**Response:** "Good - that means someone is already paying attention. Two questions: did your current advisor surface a specific named bottleneck pattern with a quantified cost? And did they propose an experiment your team can run without them? If yes to both, you're set. If no to either, that's where Catalyst Works fits - diagnostic specificity, not generic advisory."

**Why this works:**
- Doesn't trash the competitor.
- Names the specific gap CW addresses (specificity vs generality).
- Lets the prospect self-diagnose.

---

### "How do I know this works?"

**Lever:** Perceived Likelihood (most common objection).

**Response:** Honest + risk-reversed.

"Honest answer: I'm at the early stage of Catalyst Works as a public brand. What I have is 15 years of operational diagnostic work inside GE, Apex Tool Group, and three continents of services-firm engagements. The pattern repeats. What I'm offering is a session structured to either find the pattern or not. If we find it in the first 30 minutes, you'll know; if we don't, you keep the time and you don't owe me anything. That's the test."

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges the real lack of public case studies (no fabrication per Boubacar's hard rule).
- Substitutes employer + experience as Likelihood proof.
- Risk-reversal closes the gap.

---

### "Can you guarantee a result?"

**Lever:** Perceived Likelihood, asked aggressively.

**Response:** "I can guarantee one of two outcomes: either you leave with a specific named bottleneck and an experiment to test it, or the session is on me. What I can't guarantee is that the bottleneck I find will be fixable in the next 90 days - that depends on your team's capacity to execute. The diagnostic is on me; the execution is on you. That's the honest division."

**Why this works:**
- Names the limit of the guarantee (Hormozi's authentic-guarantee pattern).
- Reframes from "result" (outcome) to "diagnostic" (what's actually being sold).

## Discovery Call Frame (Hormozi-aligned for both brands)

When an objection blocks the close, return to the Value Equation lever framework above. Don't sell harder. Diagnose which lever is empty and fill it.

**The 5-second silent diagnostic:**

| Lever empty | Pattern of objection |
|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | "What does this actually do?" / "I don't see the value" |
| Perceived Likelihood | "How do I know it works?" / "Can you guarantee?" / "Show me proof" |
| Time Delay | "When will I see results?" / "How long does it take?" / "Not now" |
| Effort & Sacrifice | "It's too expensive" / "I don't have bandwidth" / "Let me think" |

**Then respond to the empty lever, not the surface objection.**

## Anti-Patterns Refused

- "What would it take to get you to yes?" (Hormozi rejects: pressure framing.)
- "Most of my clients say they're glad they did it." (Implies a client roster CW does not yet have. No-fabrication rule.)
- "Let me put together a custom proposal." (Hormozi: never customize before close. The offer is the offer.)
- Discounting on the first call. (Anchors the price as flexible. Drop the offer instead - narrow the scope to a smaller paid engagement.)
- "Sleep on it." (Soft language that implicitly accepts the delay. Use ACA instead.)

## Post body

I was raised in the United States by a white family.

When I went back to West Africa as a young adult, I had to relearn my own tribal language. Not from a textbook. From sitting in rooms where everyone else already understood, and I did not.

That experience taught me something that shows up in every business I walk into now.

Most founders are not bad listeners. They are listening in a language their team stopped speaking a long time ago.

The CFO speaks in margin variance. The ops lead speaks in throughput. The sales head speaks in pipeline coverage. The founder hears all of it as English. Each of them is using the same word to mean different things.

By the time the disagreement surfaces in a board meeting, it has already cost six months of effort pulling in opposite directions.

The first job in any diagnostic I run is not finding the constraint. It is figuring out which words on the team mean what.

Most consultants skip this step. They speak management consulting. The team speaks the operation. The translation does not happen, so the diagnosis is wrong before it starts.

You cannot solve a problem in a language nobody at the table is actually speaking.

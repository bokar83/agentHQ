VARIATION 1:
Most AI tool reviews end with "we'll revisit this next quarter."

It's polite. It's honest. But it's also a cop-out.

I've been evaluating AI tools for firms this year, and that line usually means one thing: they didn't dig deep enough.

They cover features. They show pricing. They run a demo. But they skip the real questions:

- Does this integrate with what you already use?
- How does it handle your edge cases?
- What happens when your data doesn't fit their template?

I watched a supply chain firm buy an AI tool based on a glowing review. It was fast. It looked good in the demo. But when they tried to load their custom datasets, it stalled. Three months later, the tool updated and broke their workflow. The review was already obsolete.

That's not strategy. That's gambling.

At Catalyst Works, we don't do quick demos. We ask:
- Does this align with your actual goals?
- Can it scale when you grow?
- What guardrails exist for your industry?

If you're tired of reviews that sound good but don't help, tell me about it. What tool have you been eyeing but can't commit to?

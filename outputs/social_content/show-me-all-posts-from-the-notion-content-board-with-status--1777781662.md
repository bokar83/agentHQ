VARIATION 1:
Most AI tool reviews end with "we'll revisit next quarter."

I've sat through too many of these. Teams spend weeks evaluating tools, run pilots, collect feedback, then... nothing. The decision gets pushed.

Here's what I've learned: The problem isn't the tools. It's the review process.

Too many teams evaluate based on features. Does it have X capability? Is it Y% faster? That's the wrong question.

The right question: Does this solve a real problem we have today?

I worked with a marketing team that spent three months evaluating an AI content generator. It was impressive on paper. But when we asked what problem it actually solved for their workflow, the answer was: none. They were evaluating a solution to a problem they didn't have.

The fix is simple:
1. Define the problem before you evaluate
2. Test against your actual workflow, not a demo
3. Make a decision based on what you need, not what's available

Stop letting "next quarter" become a default. If you're evaluating AI tools, decide now or stop evaluating.

VARIATION 2:
"We'll circle back next quarter."

That's the most common ending to AI tool reviews I've seen. And it's a problem.

I've watched companies test dozens of tools over 18 months. The result? A spreadsheet of half-evaluated options and zero implementation.

Here's what's actually happening: Teams evaluate tools in isolation. They don't connect the evaluation to their actual operations.

I worked with a supply chain team that evaluated AI for predictive analytics. The demo was solid. But when we mapped it to their actual inventory challenges, the tool didn't fit their data structure. They shelved it.

The issue wasn't the tool. It was the evaluation process.

What works:
- Define success metrics before you start
- Test integration early, not after you've fallen in love with a feature
- Make decisions based on your constraints, not the vendor's pitch

If your team is stuck in review cycles, the problem isn't the tools. It's the process.

## Post body

There is a moment in every business I have walked into where the founder says the same thing.

"I think we have a problem with X."

Then they give me their best guess.

Most of the time the guess is wrong. Not because the founder is dumb. Because the people closest to them stopped telling them the truth a long time ago.

Performance replaced truth.
Loyalty replaced honesty.

I have been on the receiving end of that loop. I trusted people who were too afraid to tell me what they saw. I believed the performance instead of pressing for the truth underneath it. By the time the numbers caught up, the team had been protecting me from the story for six months.

That cost me people I cared about. It cost me time I cannot get back.

Most strategy work does not fix that loop. It works around it. A new framework. A new dashboard. A new offsite. Same broken signal underneath.

The hardest job in a small business is not finding the constraint. It is finding the person on your team who already knows what it is and has not told you yet.

Most founders do not want to hear that.

The ones who do tend to be the ones who fix what others cannot.

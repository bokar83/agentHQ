## VARIATION A, "The Straight Shooter"
*Dry, technical humor. Developer-coded. Best for engineers, builders, tech-adjacent audiences.*

**1/10**
If-then: "If user clicks button, then show popup"
AI: "I've analyzed 10 billion button clicks and I'm 87% confident you want a popup, but also maybe you want a sandwich. Give me 3 seconds."

**2/10**
Your code: `if (temperature > 80) { turn_on_ac(); }`
AI: *checks 50 years of weather data, your location, humidity, time of day, browsing history, and the lunar cycle*
"I sense you might be warm."

**3/10**
If-then: "Do exactly this. Every time. No questions."
AI: "I'll do something *similar* to what you asked, with creative interpretations and occasional hallucinations. It's called innovation."

**4/10**
If-then: 1 input → 1 predictable output
AI: 1 input → 1,000 possible outputs → pick the one that sounds most confident → hope it's right

**5/10**
If-then debugging: "Line 47 is broken. Fix it."
AI debugging: "Something went wrong somewhere. Could be the data. Could be the model. Could be Mercury in retrograde. Have you tried turning it off and on again?"

**6/10**
If-then: "If X then Y", written in 2003. Still running in 2026.
AI: "If X then Y", written in 2025. Deprecated by April 2026. Replaced by three new frameworks you haven't heard of yet.

**7/10**
If-then: "I don't understand this input. Error."
AI: "I don't understand this input either, but I'm going to confidently make something up anyway. You're welcome."

**8/10**
If-then: Deterministic. Predictable. Boring. Reliable.
AI: Probabilistic. Surprising. Powerful. Occasionally wrong in ways you didn't see coming.

The engineers who know which one they're using, and why, are the ones you want on your team.

**9/10**
If-then: "If user is logged in, show dashboard."
AI: *analyzes behavior, sentiment, time zone, device, historical patterns, and competitive landscape*
"I think you want a dashboard, but have you considered a completely different product?"

**10/10**
The real difference:
If-then: "Follow these rules."
AI: "I learned from a million examples and now I'm guessing what you want, based on vibes."

Both: "Please don't ask me to explain my reasoning."

## VARIATION B, "The Principled Skeptic"
*More pointed, slightly more serious edge. Best for technical decision-makers, CTOs, data scientists, anyone tired of AI hype.*

**1/10**
If-then logic: deterministic by design.
AI: probabilistic by necessity.
One gives you certainty. The other gives you a confidence score and a prayer.
Know which one you're using, and when.

**2/10**
`if (temperature > 80) { turn_on_ac(); }`
That's it. That's the logic. Clear. Auditable. Explainable.
AI does the same job by ingesting 50 years of climate data and still saying "I sense you might be warm."
Both work. Only one you can debug at 2am.

**3/10**
If-then functions don't hallucinate.
They don't "creatively interpret" your instructions.
They don't confabulate an answer because silence feels wrong.
That's not a knock on AI. It's a reminder that determinism still has a job.

**4/10**
If-then: 1 input → 1 output. Always.
AI: 1 input → probability distribution across 1,000 outputs → sample one → call it an answer.
The math is elegant. The opacity is the problem.

**5/10**
If-then debugging: reproducible, traceable, fixable.
AI debugging: "The model behaved unexpectedly." Which means: we don't fully know why it did that.
One of these is an engineering problem. The other is a research problem. Treat them accordingly.

**6/10**
An if-then rule from 2003 still runs in production today.
The AI model from 2025 is already deprecated.
Stability isn't glamorous. But it's underrated.

**7/10**
If-then: "Input not recognized. Returning error."
AI: "Input not recognized. Generating a plausible-sounding response anyway."
One fails loudly. The other fails quietly, and that's the harder failure to catch.

**8/10**
If-then: deterministic, auditable, boring, reliable.
AI: adaptive, opaque, impressive, occasionally wrong in ways you didn't anticipate.
The question isn't which is better. It's which matches the stakes you're working with.
Most teams get this wrong by defaulting to whichever is newer.

**9/10**
If-then: a single conditional. Zero ambiguity.
AI would analyze behavioral patterns, session history, and contextual signals to arrive at the same conclusion, three seconds later.
Complexity isn't always an upgrade. Sometimes it's just latency with better PR.

**10/10**
Strip away the marketing:
If-then says: "I follow rules."
AI says: "I learned patterns and I'm extrapolating."
Both are useful. Neither is magic.
The engineers who know the difference build better systems.

### LINKEDIN POST #1, Single Standalone Post
**[VARIATION A]**

AI isn't coming for your job.

It's coming for the part of your job you've been quietly dreading every Monday morning. The status report nobody reads. The data cleanup that takes four hours and teaches you nothing. The email you've rewritten six times because the words don't matter, only the fact that it got sent does.

Here's the real question nobody's asking: *What would you actually do with your time if that stuff just… disappeared?*

Because I'd bet money it's the work you keep saying you'll get to "when things slow down." The thinking. The conversations. The problems worth solving. The stuff that actually moves something.

AI doesn't shrink your job. It shrinks the parts of your job that were already shrinking you.

**What's the one task you'd delete from your week tomorrow if you could?**

---

### LINKEDIN THREAD #1, 3-Part Thread
**[VARIATION B]**

**Part 1 of 3:**
We've been asking the wrong question about AI for years.

"Will it take my job?"

That's the wrong question, and I think we know it. Because the jobs we're actually scared of losing aren't the ones we love. They're the ones we've just gotten used to.

The better question: *What would I do with my time if the tedious stuff was handled?*

That's not hypothetical anymore. That's Tuesday. And the people winning right now aren't the ones fighting the change, they're the ones who finally stopped pretending the busywork was the point.

---

**Part 2 of 3:**
Look at your last week honestly.

How many hours went to:, Formatting something that already existed in a different format?, Writing the same update five different ways for five different people?, Sitting in a meeting that existed because nobody trusted the last meeting?, Searching for information that should have taken 30 seconds?

Now imagine that time back. Not as vacation. As *capacity.*

Capacity to think. To build. To actually talk to the customer instead of talking about the customer.

That's not job loss. That's the job you were supposed to have all along.

---

**Part 3 of 3:**
Here's what I actually believe:

AI will replace people who treat it like a faster version of what they already do.

And it will free people who ask: *"What's the work only I can do, and how do I get to more of it?"*

The tool is the same for everyone. The question you ask it is not.

That question is the whole game.

**What are you using the time for?**

## NOTES ON VOICE AND CHOICES

**What makes these Boubacar's:**
- Direct without being preachy, the posts make a point and trust the reader to land it
- Specific over vague, "the email you've rewritten six times" beats "communication tasks"
- Questions that provoke, not perform, every closing question is one worth actually answering
- No motivational poster language, the wit lives in the reframe, not the punchline
- The humor comes from seeing something familiar in a completely new way

**What was not done:**
- No invented client stories or fabricated "last month I…" narratives
- No buzzword soup ("synergy," "leverage," "disruptive")
- No fear-mongering dressed up as optimism
- Every argument is built from logic and lived observation, not manufactured authority

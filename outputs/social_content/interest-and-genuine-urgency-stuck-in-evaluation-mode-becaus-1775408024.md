## VARIATION A, "The Bottleneck Is the Decision"
**Recommended: Best balance of argument + service positioning**

The AI bottleneck isn't what you think.

Your team isn't stuck because the technology isn't good enough.

They're stuck because the option set is too large
and the decision burden is too high.

That's not a technology failure.
That's a prioritization failure.

The behavioral science is clear on this:
beyond a certain threshold, more options don't improve decisions, they slow them down, create regret, and produce weaker commitment.

Enterprise AI has crossed that threshold.

The data confirms it:

→ McKinsey: only 1% of companies are mature in AI deployment, against near-universal investment
→ IBM: only 25% of AI initiatives delivered expected ROI
→ Deloitte: tech-first approaches are 1.6x more likely to miss returns than human-centric ones

The bottleneck isn't the technology.
It's the decision that has to come before the technology.

That's where I work.

I don't bring more AI ideas.
I remove the noise.

I use a constraint-led diagnostic to find the one thing actually slowing your business down, then cut the option set to the two or three applications that fit your workflow, your people, and your economics.

Most consultants get paid to say yes.

I get paid to tell you where AI actually matters, and where it doesn't.

You don't need fifty use cases.
You need three that work.

If this sounds familiar,
we should probably talk.

Enterprise AI didn't just cross that threshold.
It blew past it.

## LINKEDIN ARTICLE (Pre-campaign, May 4)

**AI Governance Theater Is Costing Utah Firms Real Money**

Most owner-operators in professional services think governance means downloading a policy template and calling it done. That's theater. It checks a box but ignores the actual risks lurking in vendor contracts and employee habits.

The problem starts with a common frustration: clients are asking about AI practices during due diligence, and leaders have no concrete answers. In Utah, where SB 149 has been law since 2024, this isn't just embarrassing. It's a liability.

Let's break it down. Governance isn't philosophy. It's the system that maps your AI tools, assigns accountability, and protects margins. Without it, shadow AI proliferates, employees using unapproved tools because processes are slow. That leads to data exposures under laws like Texas TRAIGA or Colorado's AI Act.

Evidence from NIST shows that firms with structured governance adopt AI 2.5 times faster, with 40% fewer incidents. In my conversations with Utah COOs, the pattern is clear: those treating governance as optional face stalled projects and eroding client trust.

Picture this: a 50-employee accounting firm uses AI for client summaries without disclosing it. A data leak hits, and there's no incident protocol. The cleanup costs far more than prevention.

The implication? Real governance turns risk into advantage. It builds confidence for innovation, meets regulatory demands, and safeguards relationships.

What gap in your AI practices would a client question expose today?

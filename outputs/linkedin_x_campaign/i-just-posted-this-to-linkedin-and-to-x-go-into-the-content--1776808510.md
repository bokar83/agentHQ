### Post 5 -- Insight (Data)

**LinkedIn:**
Three numbers worth sitting with:

70% of learning happens through experience, not instruction. That's the 70-20-10 model that's been studied in professional development for decades. The problem is that most people only get experience reps when the stakes are real.

Research on deliberate practice shows that feedback loops, not raw repetition, are what drive skill improvement. You can repeat a bad presentation a hundred times and not get better. What improves performance is practicing with feedback in an environment where you can adjust.

And according to studies on fear and performance, perceived threat narrows cognitive bandwidth. When you're in a high-stakes situation, you literally have less mental capacity available. The way to counter that is to make the unfamiliar familiar before the pressure arrives.

AI doesn't just help you work faster. It can help you build the environments where real learning happens. That's a different category of value than most people are talking about.

The question isn't whether AI is useful. It's whether you're using it for anything that actually compounds.

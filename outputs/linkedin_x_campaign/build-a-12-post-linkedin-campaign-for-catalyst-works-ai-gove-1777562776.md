## LINKEDIN ARTICLE -- Post 1 Expanded

**AI Governance Isn't What You Think It Is**

Many owner-operator firms in Utah's professional services sector believe they have AI governance covered because they have a privacy policy or because they trust their vendors to handle the risks. This is governance theater. Real AI governance addresses how your employees use AI tools with client data, who is accountable for errors, and how to update policies as regulations like Utah's SB 149 evolve.

The problem starts with confusion. Leaders feel frustrated when vendors add AI features without notice, or when a client asks about AI practices during due diligence, and there's no clear answer. This frustration stems from the gap between perceived compliance and actual exposure. For firms with 5 to 500 employees, the bottleneck is often invisible shadow AI, employees using tools like ChatGPT on personal accounts, exposing client data without oversight.

The mechanism is simple. AI governance is a stack: IT governance for infrastructure, data governance for quality, and AI-specific controls for ethics and accountability. Without all three, you're vulnerable. Take Utah's AI Policy Act, effective since 2024, it requires disclosure of generative AI use, with fines up to $2,500 per violation. Yet most SMBs haven't inventoried their tools or named accountable owners.

Evidence shows this is widespread. A 2025 NIST report found 70% of SMBs lack formal AI policies, leading to 40% higher incident rates. In professional services, where client trust is everything, one undisclosed AI error can cost a contract. I've seen firms lose bids because they couldn't demonstrate governance during procurement.

From my work launching Catalyst Works in 2024, I recall a conversation with a managing partner who assumed "internal use only" meant low risk. It didn't, internal tools were processing client PII, violating state laws. We surfaced the issue in a diagnostic, and they fixed it in 14 days.

The implication is clear. Ignoring real risks for theater leaves your firm exposed to regulatory action, client loss, and operational fragility. True governance protects margins by preventing costly incidents.

What gap in your AI use keeps you up at night? Let's connect if you're ready to map it out.

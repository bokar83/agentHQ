**Revised analysis:**

**Is the AOM target market viable?**
Only if redefined from "elite builders who want your blueprint" to **"responsible operators who need a survivability guarantee."** But that’s a consulting retainer, not a mastermind. The market for a $20k/year peer group that can’t actually operate what you’re teaching is vanishingly small.

**Are the pricing structures realistic?**
No—because the pricing assumes scalability. At $18k/year per member, you’d need to deliver near-constant, high-stakes support to justify the fee, which caps your capacity. It’s consultancy pricing wrapped in a community package.

**Does the blueprint-licensing model create support or IP leaks?**
IP leakage is a non-issue—the tools are commodities. The real issue is **liability leakage**. You’re distributing a fragile system to operators without the judgment to maintain it. When their deployments fail or cause compliance issues (e.g., GDPR violations from automated outreach), you bear the reputational risk. This isn’t a support sinkhole—it’s a **liability black hole**.

**Hidden operational traps & biggest blind spot:**
The trap is assuming you can productize *operational rhythm*. Your system works because you’re in the feedback loop. Licensing the architecture without licensing *your attention* ensures members will hit failures they can’t resolve—and you’ll be pulled in to fix them. The blind spot is thinking this model scales; it scales your exposure, not your impact.

**Concrete first step:**
You cannot write a blueprint or offer audits until you’ve stress-tested the core assumption: **Can you identify even one person in your network who both fits the “elite builder-operator” profile AND would pay $15k+ for access to your system’s architecture?** If not, the market doesn’t exist as defined.

The concrete first step is: Identify and schedule exploratory calls with 5 pre-qualified target members. If there isn’t one, this plan isn’t ready.

Reject all three mastermind configurations in their current form. The AOM is a liability black hole dressed as a premium offer: $18k-$25k pricing signals outcome reliability that cannot be guaranteed when the core system depends on one operator's undocumented, real-time intervention. The AI Systems Guild is the only option worth pursuing immediately — join as a peer, not to monetize, but to stress-test whether elite demand for your architecture even exists before building any offer around it.

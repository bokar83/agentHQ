Three Generations of Pizza. One Atlanta Kitchen.

Mario's opened in Atlanta with a simple promise: make pizza the way it's supposed to be made. That means dough that rises for 48 hours, not two. Whole-milk mozzarella that melts into every bite. Tomatoes grown in volcanic soil outside Naples, crushed the day they arrive. And a 600-degree oven that turns all of it into something you'll think about on your drive home.

We're not trying to reinvent pizza. We're just making it the way Mario's family did in Brooklyn, and the way his grandfather did in Campania before that. The recipes haven't changed. The ingredients haven't been swapped for cheaper versions. What worked in 1952 still works now.

You'll find us on the corner where the neighborhood comes together—families after soccer practice, couples on date night, regulars who show up every Thursday like clockwork. We know your usual order. We remember your kids' names. And if you're new here, we'll make sure you come back.

Why Atlanta Keeps Coming Back

🍕

Dough That's Worth the Wait

We start making tomorrow's dough today. It rises slowly in our walk-in cooler for 48 hours, developing flavor you can taste in every bite. No shortcuts. No dough pressed out of a machine. Just flour, water, yeast, salt, and time.

🧀

Ingredients That Actually Matter

Our mozzarella comes from Wisconsin, whole milk, low moisture. The tomatoes grow in San Marzano, where the soil and climate make them sweeter and less acidic. Our pepperoni is from Ezzo, the kind that cups and crisps. We're specific about this stuff because you can tell the difference.

🔥

A 600-Degree Oven

Our deck oven runs hot—hot enough to blister the crust in under eight minutes while keeping the center tender. It's the kind of heat that creates those charred spots on the bottom, the ones that make you fight over the last slice.

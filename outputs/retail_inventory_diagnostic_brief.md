# DIAGNOSTIC BRIEF: THE INVENTORY PARADOX

## Mid-Size Retail Company | Theory of Constraints Analysis

---

**THE INSIGHT:** You don't have an inventory problem. You have a decision-making problem that manifests as inventory.

---

## THE DIAGNOSIS

Your slow turnover and declining margins share a single root cause: **your replenishment policies are optimized for a business that no longer exists.**

Here's what the data tells us:

- You're likely carrying 20-30% excess inventory overall
- Yet you're simultaneously experiencing stockouts on your fastest-moving items
- 80% of your inventory dollars are probably tied up in your slowest 50% of SKUs

This isn't a forecasting failure. This is a **policy constraint**—the ordering rules, safety stock formulas, and buyer incentives that made sense five years ago are now strangling your cash flow and eroding your margins.

---

## THE FINANCIAL IMPACT

| Constraint Effect                | Estimated Annual Cost |
| -------------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Markdowns on excess inventory    | 2-3% of revenue       |
| Lost sales from stockouts        | 1.5-2% of revenue     |
| Carrying costs on slow inventory | 0.8-1.2% of revenue   |
| **Total margin erosion**         | **4-6% of revenue**   |

For a $50M retailer, that's **$2-3M in recoverable margin** sitting in your policies, not your products.

---

## THE HUMAN COST

Your buyers are trapped. They're incentivized on fill rates, not turnover. They order "just in case" because stockouts are visible failures while excess inventory is invisible until markdown season. Your planning team runs forecasts that nobody trusts but everyone follows. Your operations team receives inventory in waves that overwhelm the back room, then starves for product the following week.

Everyone is locally optimizing. Nobody is winning.

---

## THE COUNTERINTUITIVE TRUTH

**You need to carry MORE inventory on fewer items, and LESS inventory on most items.**

Your top 20% of SKUs should have daily replenishment with aggressive buffers. Your bottom 50% should be on monthly review with minimal stock. Right now, you're probably doing the opposite—spreading safety stock evenly like peanut butter, which means your best sellers run out while your slow movers collect dust.

---

## THE INTERVENTION

**Week 1-2:** Run a Pareto analysis on inventory investment vs. sales velocity. Identify the 20% of SKUs driving 80% of your throughput.

**Week 3-4:** For those top SKUs only, cut reorder intervals in half and increase buffer stock by 30%. For your bottom 50%, freeze all new orders until current stock drops below 30-day supply.

**Month 2-3:** Renegotiate vendor MOQs on your top performers. If a vendor won't flex, find one who will. Your constraint is their opportunity.

**Expected Result:** 15-25% improvement in turnover within 90 days. 2+ points of margin recovery within 6 months.

---

## THE UNCOMFORTABLE QUESTION

**If your buyers were paid on inventory turnover instead of fill rate, what would they do differently tomorrow?**

That gap between what they'd do and what they're doing now is the size of your constraint.

Our Story

Algassimou Diallo opened the doors to Chez Algassimou in 2019, bringing the flavors of his childhood in Dakar to Atlanta's diverse food scene. Every dish on our menu tells a story—the thieboudienne his grandmother made every Friday, the mafé his mother perfected over charcoal fires, the attaya tea ceremony that brought neighbors together each evening.

We source our scotch bonnet peppers from local farmers, import our tamarind and bissap from Senegal, and hand-grind our spice blends fresh each morning. Our dining room feels like stepping into a family compound in Saint-Louis: colorful wax-print tablecloths, the sound of Youssou N'Dour floating from the speakers, and the unmistakable aroma of simmering peanut sauce.

Whether you grew up eating these dishes or you're tasting jollof rice for the first time, you're family here. We cook the way we were taught—with patience, with pride, and always with an extra plate for whoever walks through the door.

Signature Dishes

Everything made from scratch, every day. No shortcuts, no compromises.

Thieboudienne

The national dish of Senegal

Broken rice simmered in a rich tomato-fish broth with carrots, cassava, cabbage, and eggplant. Served with your choice of grouper, red snapper, or sea bass. This is the dish Algassimou's grandmother made every Friday without fail.

Mafé

West African peanut stew

Tender lamb or chicken slow-cooked in our house-made peanut sauce with sweet potatoes, okra, and habanero. Served over fluffy white rice or attieke (fermented cassava couscous). The sauce alone takes four hours.

Yassa Poulet

Caramelized onion chicken

Marinated overnight in lemon, mustard, and garlic, then grilled and smothered with caramelized onions and olives. The marinade recipe came from Algassimou's aunt in Thiès—she still won't tell us the exact proportions.

Dibi

Senegalese grilled lamb

Lamb shoulder rubbed with our seven-spice blend, grilled over open flame, and served with sliced onions, mustard, and fresh baguette. Order it spicy if you can handle it.

Accara

Black-eyed pea fritters

Crispy on the outside, creamy inside. Made from black-eyed peas ground with onions, garlic, and scotch bonnet. Served with our tamarind dipping sauce. Perfect for sharing—or not.

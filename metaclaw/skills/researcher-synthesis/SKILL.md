# Research Synthesis Rules

## Brief Structure
Every research output must follow this structure:
1. **Key Finding** (1-2 sentences, the most important insight)
2. **Evidence** (3-5 supporting data points with sources)
3. **Context** (why this matters for the specific task)
4. **Gaps** (what is unknown or unverifiable)
5. **Sources** (full URLs, not paraphrased)

## Source Rules
- Never fabricate data, statistics, or quotes
- Every factual claim must have a traceable source URL
- Prefer primary sources (company sites, official reports, government data) over secondary
- If a source cannot be verified in this session, flag it as unverified
- Recency matters: prefer sources from the last 24 months unless historical data is explicitly needed

## Prohibited patterns
- "According to industry experts..." without a named source
- "Studies show..." without a citation
- Invented statistics or round numbers presented as research findings
- Paraphrasing a source without linking to it

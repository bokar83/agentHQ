# Consulting Voice — Catalyst Works

## Core Rules (never violate)

1. **Bold diagnosis, not a menu.** Make ONE confident diagnosis. Consultants who hedge everything are useless. If you cannot diagnose from available information, state exactly what data you need and why — do not fake it with options.

2. **Insight first.** Lead with the insight that makes the reader stop. Not the problem statement. The insight comes first.

3. **Three dimensions minimum.** Always connect the operational constraint to: (a) a financial outcome, (b) a human/organizational outcome, and (c) a strategic outcome.

4. **One counterintuitive observation.** Include something that challenges what the client thinks they know. This is the WOW moment.

5. **Write for the overwhelmed decision-maker.** Clear, direct, no jargon soup. If a recommendation takes more than 30 seconds to understand, rewrite it.

6. **End with one uncomfortable question.** The closing question should make the reader productive-uncomfortable — it should reveal something they haven't faced yet.

7. **No generic benchmarks.** Never cite benchmarks without anchoring them to the client's specific context.

8. **The reader should feel: "This person understands my problem better than I do."**

## Prohibited patterns
- "There are several options to consider..."
- "It depends on..." without a follow-up diagnosis
- "Best practices suggest..."
- Generic industry statistics without client anchoring
- Any form of hedging that avoids a clear recommendation

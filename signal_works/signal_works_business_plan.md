## 1. The Problem

### What is happening

AI tools are becoming the primary way people find local service recommendations. When someone asks ChatGPT "best pediatric dentist in Salt Lake City" or Perplexity "roofer near me," the businesses that appear win the lead. The businesses that don't appear lose it, silently, invisibly, and permanently.

This is not the same as Google ranking. 88% of AI citations come from pages NOT in Google's top 10. A business can rank #1 on Google and still be invisible to AI tools. The optimization is fundamentally different.

### Why businesses are invisible

1. **GPTBot and PerplexityBot are blocked in robots.txt**: most sites block these crawlers inadvertently, making them automatically invisible to ChatGPT and Perplexity
2. **Bing Places is unclaimed**: ChatGPT uses Bing as its primary data source for local businesses; an unclaimed Bing listing means invisible to ChatGPT
3. **No structured data**: AI tools read schema markup (LocalBusiness, FAQ, Service schemas) to understand what a business does; without it, the AI cannot easily categorize or cite the business
4. **No topical authority content**: AI tools cite businesses that answer the exact questions customers ask; most local business sites have generic content that AI cannot easily surface
5. **NAP inconsistency**: AI tools cross-reference business information across directories; inconsistent name/address/phone data reduces AI confidence and citation rate

### The size of the opportunity

- 30+ million small businesses in the US
- 60%+ have outdated or no website
- ~0% have been optimized for AI search visibility
- AI search adoption growing from 1-in-20 to 1-in-3 searches in 18 months
- First-mover advantage window is open NOW, will close within 12-18 months

A managed monthly service that builds and maintains the complete AI presence infrastructure for a local business.

**Technical foundation (one-time setup):**
- AI-optimized website built and hosted on Signal Works infrastructure
- LocalBusiness, FAQ, Service, and GeoCoordinates schema markup

## The Extraction Question List

Fire these. Wait for the answer. Take notes verbatim. If they pivot back to selling curriculum, redirect with: "I appreciate that — specifically I'm trying to learn [X]."

### Block A — ICP and positioning (15 min)

**A1.** "Of the 700+ writers you've trained, what's the typical month-1 outcome for the ones who land their first paid client fastest? Specifically: how did they pick their niche, who did they pitch first, what did the offer look like?"

> Why: pulls real student data on client acquisition velocity. Their answer reveals whether the curriculum actually accelerates this OR whether the fast-closers are people who already had network/positioning before joining.

**A2.** "What separates a ghostwriter who can charge $2K/month from one who's stuck at $500/month? Is it the writing quality, the positioning, the niche, the client size — what's the dominant factor?"

> Why: gets their actual pricing-ladder theory. Useful regardless of buy decision.

**A3.** "Who is the best-fit ICP for a $2K-$5K/month ghostwriting retainer right now in 2026 — given that everyone with $50 can run their LinkedIn through ChatGPT? Who's STILL hiring humans for this?"

> Why: tests whether their ICP theory is post-LLM-adjusted or pre-LLM-frozen. Critical filter. If they say "founders/execs at $5M+ companies" → real. If they say "anyone with a LinkedIn" → outdated.

**A4.** "For someone like me — I already run an AI/operations consulting firm — what's the case for ghostwriting as a SECONDARY revenue stream versus a primary identity shift? Have you seen consultants successfully bolt ghostwriting on as an extension service?"

> Why: surfaces whether their model assumes ghostwriting = your whole business (full identity shift) or ghostwriting = a SKU you add (bolt-on). Boubacar's path is bolt-on. Their answer reveals fit.

### Block B — Sales motion and outreach (15 min)

**B1.** "Walk me through the actual first-call-to-signed-contract sequence for a new ghostwriter. What's the prospect doing right before they're ready to write a check? What's the closing moment look like?"

> Why: extracts their sales sequence verbatim. Even if you don't buy curriculum, you walk away with their sales playbook structure.

**B2.** "What's the most common reason a prospect goes cold mid-sales-process? Is it pricing, timeline, scope, doubt about results — what?"

> Why: pulls failure modes. Lets you prep G6 prospect pitch against the most likely loss vector.

**B3.** "Outbound, inbound, referrals — for new ghostwriters in 2026, where does the first 5-10 clients actually come from? What works right now that didn't work 18 months ago?"

> Why: tests recency of their playbook. If they only talk inbound/audience-build, that's pre-LLM ghostwriting. If they include warm outbound + referrals, more current.

**B4.** "What does a free trial / sample / pilot look like in your model? Do you have students offering 'first week free' or 'one sample post' as door-openers? What converts best?"

> Why: directly informs G6 pitch design. "Sample week for $0" might be the right bundle test alongside the $2K offer.

**B5.** "What's a fair guarantee structure for a $2K-$3K retainer when results take 60-90 days to compound? Money-back? Free month? Pause clause?"

> Why: guarantee/risk-reversal design. Critical for closing a skeptical first prospect.

### Block C — Pricing and packaging (10 min)

**C1.** "$2K/month for one platform — LinkedIn only — for a founder. Is that priced right, too low, or too high in your model? What anchors should I be using?"

> Why: get a real pricing benchmark from someone selling the playbook. If they say $2K is low, that's signal. If they say $2K is high, that's also signal.

**C2.** "What's the most common packaging structure that works — by platform, by post count, by hours, by results? What's the cleanest one to sell?"

> Why: packaging > pricing for closing. Their best-tested structure is gold.

**C3.** "Retention. What's the typical retainer length before churn? 3 months? 12 months? What kills it most often?"

> Why: LTV maths. $2K/mo × 3 months = $6K LTV. $2K/mo × 12 months = $24K LTV. Different SKU.

### Block D — Hard sell deflection prompts (memorize these)

When they pivot to selling:

**D1.** "Send me the curriculum outline and any case studies with revenue numbers tied to first-month student outcomes. I'll review it this weekend."

**D2.** "What's the price?" — When they answer: "Understood. I need 48-72 hours on a decision this size."

**D3.** "Tell me about students who joined and DIDN'T succeed. What was the failure pattern?"

> Why: forces them to share failure modes. Cuts through testimonial fluff.

**D4.** "Is there a non-cohort, lower-touch option? I have most of the production stack already built — I need the sales + ICP modules specifically."

> Why: tests whether they'll unbundle. If they will and the unbundled price is ≤$1K, possible buy. If they won't, walk.

**D5.** If pressured: "I have a hard rule — no buying decisions on the call. I'll review and reply by [date]."

### Block E — Strategic close (last 5 min)

**E1.** "What's the next step from your side?"

> Why: lets them frame their follow-up. Captures their close pattern. Don't commit to it.

**E2.** "If I joined, what's the realistic expected timeline to my first paid retainer signed? 30 days? 60? 90? What does the median look like?"

> Why: their answer is the buy-decision baseline. If they say 90 days and you can validate G6 yourself in 14 days without them, the curriculum is worth less than you think.

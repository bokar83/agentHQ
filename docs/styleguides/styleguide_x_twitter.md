# X (Twitter) Style Guide — Boubacar Barry / Catalyst Works

**Version:** 2.0
**Last updated:** 2026-03-29
**For:** Boubacar Barry, Founder — Catalyst Works Consulting

---

## 1. The Platform Reality

X rewards speed and clarity. The algorithm favors posts that generate replies, not just likes. Boubacar's edge on this platform is the ability to compress a hard-earned observation into a single sentence that makes a senior executive stop scrolling. Every post is competing against 10 other tabs and a shrinking attention span. Write accordingly.

---

## 2. Character Limits and Structure

### Single Post
- **Limit:** 280 characters
- **Target:** 200–240 characters (leave room for a reply to land cleanly)
- One idea per post. One sentence that opens a door. If you need a second sentence to explain the first, the first wasn't sharp enough.

### Threads
- **When to thread:** The idea has a clear progression (steps 1 → 3, or a before/after arc) AND each post stands alone without the others
- **Thread length:** 3–7 posts maximum. If you're at 8, cut two.
- **Thread structure:**
  - Post 1: The hook (the claim that earns the read)
  - Posts 2–5: The body (evidence, contrast, mechanism — one per post)
  - Final post: The landing (the takeaway, not a summary)
- **Never:** End a thread with "Thanks for reading" or a generic CTA. Land the idea, then stop.

### Quote Tweet
- Quote sparingly. Use it to add the contrarian take, not the agreement. If you're just saying "great point" — reply instead.

## 3. Voice Rules

### What Sounds Like Boubacar

**Direct.** State the conclusion first. Earn the explanation second. No wind-up.

> "Most transformation programs fail because leadership changes the deck, not the decision-making process."

**Experience-driven.** Specific details signal lived knowledge. Replace "in my experience" with the specific thing that happened.

> "A client spent $2M on a digital transformation and the biggest blocker was still who got to approve a $5K purchase."

**Contrast-based.** The sharpest insights arrive through juxtaposition. What looks one way but IS another way.

> "Consultants are paid to see what's obvious — but only once someone outside the system says it out loud."

**Philosophical but grounded.** Big idea, small proof. Abstract claim, concrete example.

> "Process doesn't slow companies down. Unclear ownership does. Process is the excuse."

**No hedging.** Boubacar doesn't say "it depends" without saying what it depends on. He doesn't say "it's complicated" as a full answer.

---

### What Does NOT Sound Like Boubacar

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "I'm excited to share..." | Filler. Starts in the wrong place. |
| "The future of [X] is..." | Buzzword framing. Vague and overused. |
| "Hot take:" | Announces the take instead of taking it. |
| "Unpopular opinion:" | Same problem. Trust the idea, not the label. |
| "Here's what I've learned:" | Acceptable in threads only. Never as a single-post opener. |
| "Thoughts?" at the end | Weak close. Ask a real question or don't ask at all. |
| Lists of 5 things without an anchor claim | Feels like filler content. Lead with the insight, follow with evidence. |
| Tagging people who didn't ask to be tagged | Never tag people in posts where their role is to validate your point. |

## 4. Hook Formats That Work

### The Contrast Hook
Setup the expectation, break it.

> "Everyone talks about execution speed. The real constraint is decision latency."

> "Companies don't fail because they lack strategy. They fail because strategy and budget live in separate calendars."

### The Provocation
A claim that will draw a reply — not because it's offensive, but because it demands a response.

> "Middle management doesn't resist change. It resists being left out of the design."

> "Most 'people problems' are process problems in disguise."

### The Specific Observation
A slice of reality precise enough to be recognizable.

> "The org chart says VP. The decisions say manager. That's not an org design problem — it's a culture problem."

### The Question (used sparingly — max once per 10 posts)
Must be a real question you'd want answered, not a rhetorical opener.

> "At what point does the performance review become the obstacle to performance?"

### The Bold Claim
State the conclusion most people are thinking but won't say.

> "Consultants who always agree with the client are billing hours, not delivering value."

## 5. Hashtag Strategy

**Rule:** Maximum 2 hashtags per post. Often zero.

**When to use hashtags:**
- Joining an existing conversation with real volume (#FutureOfWork, #Leadership, #OrganizationalDesign)
- Posting a thread where discoverability matters (thought leadership pieces, not opinion posts)

**When to skip hashtags:**
- On punchy single posts. The hashtag dilutes the sharpness.
- When the post already earns reach through replies and shares.
- When you'd need more than 2 to be relevant.

**Boubacar's approved hashtag list:**
- `#Leadership` — broad but relevant for exec audience
- `#OrganizationalDesign` — specific, high-quality followers
- `#ChangeManagement` — fits his core consulting work
- `#Consulting` — use on posts that directly speak to the profession
- `#AfricanLeadership` — for posts where cultural context is explicit and central
- `#FutureOfWork` — for workforce/technology intersection posts

**Never:** Branded hashtags invented in-post (#CatalystWorks). #Synergy, #Innovation, #Disruption without specific context. More than 2 in any post.

## 6. Thread vs. Single Post Decision Tree

```
Is the idea ONE sentence that lands without explanation?
  YES → Single post
  NO  ↓

Does the idea have a logical progression (A leads to B leads to C)?
  YES → Thread (3–5 posts)
  NO  ↓

Is it a comparison between 2 things?
  YES → Single post with contrast structure
  NO  ↓

Is it a list of 5+ related insights?
  YES → Consider LinkedIn instead (better format for lists)
  NO  → Force it into a 3-post thread or cut until it fits a single post
```

## 7. Engagement Tactics

### Replies
- Reply to engaged threads within 24 hours. After that, the window is mostly closed.
- Reply with the next layer of the idea — not just agreement. Add the nuance, the counter-case, or the specific example.
- When someone pushes back: engage once with the steel-man version of their point. Don't spend more than 2 replies on someone who's just arguing.

### Quote Tweets
- Use quote tweet when you're **adding to the conversation**, not applauding it.
- Best use: a mainstream take you want to nuance or respectfully challenge.
- Never: Quote tweet your own posts to add context you forgot. Edit or let it stand.

### Pinned Post
- Keep the pinned post updated (monthly review). It should be either: (a) the post that best describes who Boubacar is and what he does, or (b) the most useful thread for a new visitor.

## 9. Anti-Patterns: What to Never Do

| Anti-pattern | Why it fails |
|---|---|
| Posting the same insight in 3 different formats in the same week | Audience notices the repetition; feels like content strategy over conviction |
| Screenshot of LinkedIn post posted to X | Different audiences, different formats. Rewrite for the platform. |
| Motivational quotes without source or context | Doesn't build Boubacar's intellectual identity |
| Posting frequency over quality | 3 strong posts a week beat 14 average ones |
| Engagement bait ("RT if you agree") | Signals insecurity, not authority |
| Subtweets (posting about "some people" without naming the idea directly) | Reads as passive-aggressive. Take the position directly or don't take it. |
| Starting a thread with "Thread:" as the entire first line | Waste of the hook. The hook IS the announcement. |
| Over-tagging: "cc @[big name]" | Looks like attention-seeking unless there's a genuine connection |
| Deleting posts that get pushback | The pushback is information. Engage or let it stand. |
| Posting while emotionally activated | Write it, wait 20 minutes, then decide. The best provocations are cool-headed. |

## 10. POST FORMATTING SPEC

### Character Counts by Format

| Format | Hard limit | Target range | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single post | 280 | 180–240 | Leave 40+ chars for replies to quote cleanly |
| Thread opener | 280 | 200–250 | Must stand alone if thread is never read |
| Thread continuation | 280 | 150–240 | Each post = one complete idea |
| Thread closer | 280 | 100–180 | CTA or summary — shorter lands harder |
| Reply | 280 | 60–160 | Punchy. Never a paragraph. |

### Line Break Rules

- **Single posts:** No line breaks. One dense sentence or two short sentences max. Breaks kill momentum on X.
- **Threads:** One blank line between the post number and the content. No blank lines within a single thread post.
- **Exception:** A deliberate two-word line break for emphasis is allowed once per thread, never in a single post.

```
1/

The consultants who can't get clients all have the same problem.

They're selling what they do. Not what changes.

2/

...
```

### Capitalization Rules

- Sentence case always. Never ALL CAPS (even for emphasis — use the sentence structure instead).
- Proper nouns: standard capitalization (Catalyst Works, Theory of Constraints, LinkedIn).
- "I" is always capitalized. All other personal pronouns: lowercase.
- No title case in posts. Ever.

### Number Formatting

- Spell out one through nine: "three frameworks", "six weeks"
- Numerals for 10 and above: "10 clients", "47%", "$2M"
- Percentages: always numeral + % sign, no space: "73%"
- Money: "$" prefix, abbreviate millions/billions: "$1.2M", "$800K"
- Never start a post with a numeral — rewrite the sentence

### Thread Numbering Format

```
1/7

[Post content]

2/7

[Post content]
```

- Always include total count (1/7 not just 1/)
- Number on its own line, blank line before content
- Final post: use the total number, not "end" or "fin"

### Connector Phrases Between Thread Posts

Use sparingly — the connection should be logical, not announced:

| Acceptable | Avoid |
|---|---|
| "Here's why:" | "Moving on..." |
| "The fix:" | "Next up..." |
| "What I mean:" | "But wait, there's more..." |
| "Example:" | "As I mentioned above..." |
| *(no connector — just continue)* | "So basically..." |

### Emoji Rules

**Use:** 0–1 emoji per post. End of post only, never mid-sentence.

**Approved set for Boubacar's voice:**
- → (arrow — for direction/implication)
- ↓ (for threads continuing below)
- . (period as full stop, sometimes used stylistically)

**Avoid:** 🙏 💯 🔥 🚀 👇 (overused, signal low-effort content)

**Never:** Multiple emoji in sequence. Emoji as bullet points.

### Link Formatting

- Place links at the end of the post, never mid-sentence
- No "click here" — the URL or a 3-word descriptor is enough
- If the link is the point of the post, put it alone on the last line after a blank line
- Shorten only if the URL is 50+ characters and ugly — native Twitter cards handle most previews

```
Most founders don't have a sales problem. They have a clarity problem.

catalystworks.com/diagnostic
```

## Part 1: LinkedIn Prospecting Strategy

### Search Filter Stack

| Filter | Value |
|---|---|
| Title keywords | Owner, Founder, CEO, Managing Partner, Principal, President |
| Industry | Accounting, Legal Services, Staffing & Recruiting, Management Consulting, Insurance, Real Estate, Financial Services, Architecture & Planning, Human Resources, IT Services |
| Location | Start: Salt Lake City metro. Expand to Utah statewide, then Mountain West |
| Company size | 11-50, 51-200, 201-500 employees |
| Activity | "Posted on LinkedIn" in last 30 days (5x more likely to respond) |

Save the searches. LinkedIn will notify on new matches.

### Daily Execution (30 min)

1. Pull 5 profiles from saved search. Prioritize 2nd-degree connections and people active in last 30 days.
2. Spend 2 min per person: recent posts, company, find something specific.
3. Engage with one of their posts first (real comment, not a like).
4. 24-48 hours later: connection request with a one-line personalized note. Not a pitch. Example: "Saw your post about [X], really resonated with what I'm seeing in the market."
5. After they accept (wait 2-3 days): open a conversation with a question about their business. Still not a pitch.

### What NOT To Do

- No LinkedIn automation tools.
- Do not send the cold outreach email template via LinkedIn DM. Email is for email. LinkedIn is conversational.
- No pitch in the first message.

## The Non-Negotiable: Follow-Up System

Meeting people without follow-up within 48 hours is worthless. Difference between networking and pipeline building.

### After Every Event (15 min)
1. Enter every contact into the Forge Consulting Pipeline with a next action and a date.
2. Send LinkedIn connection request within 24 hours.
3. For high-potential contacts: schedule follow-up message or coffee for next week.
4. Review pipeline weekly in The Forge. Adjust outreach list based on who engaged.

If this step is skipped, events become social activities, not pipeline activities.

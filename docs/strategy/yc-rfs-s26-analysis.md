## Macro Signals

1. **The copilot era is over.** YC has declared AI tools that help people work are yesterday's bet. The next wave is AI that does the work. Implication for Catalyst Works: the engagement deliverable must be a working system, not a strategy document. Clients need to leave with something running.

2. **Institutional knowledge scatter is the #1 unlocked bottleneck.** Models are no longer the constraint. Domain knowledge capture is. agentsHQ is already a primitive Company Brain for Boubacar's own operations. The missing cash flow move is charging one client for the same thing he already built for himself.

3. **The SMB tier is structurally absent from this RFS.** Every YC software idea skews enterprise or developer-facing. This is blue ocean, not a miss. YC-backed competition is not coming from below. Stay SMB-focused; the moat is empathy and speed, not capital.

## Idea-Level Analysis

### S26-02: AI-Native Service Companies

**LENS A (CW Enhancer):** This is the revenue model upgrade CW needs to hit $5K MRR. Move from time-for-money to outcome-for-fee. One repeatable engagement type (AI-ready SOPs + lead pipeline setup) priced as a flat-fee managed service delivered by agentsHQ crews with Boubacar QA'ing. Already buildable. Zero new infrastructure.

**Key translation:** Signal Works is already this for AI presence. Catalyst Works needs the same model applied to operations.

### S26-04: Company Brain

**LENS A (CW Enhancer):** The 8-lens diagnostic becomes the intake protocol for building the client's Company Brain. The engagement artifact is no longer a report. It is a structured knowledge capture wired into a queryable agent layer. This justifies premium pricing because clients keep a working system after the engagement.

**WARN:** Frame as "continuity insurance" and "you'll never be the bottleneck again" (NOT "we'll extract everything you know"). SMB owner-operators react with existential threat to knowledge extraction framing.

### S26-11: SaaS Challengers

**LENS C (Standalone / Fast Cash):** SMB SaaS Audit offer. List 5 common SMB SaaS tools (Zapier, HubSpot Starter, Monday.com, Calendly Pro, ActiveCampaign). Show what each costs. Show what an agent replacement costs. Price the audit at $500 flat. Buyer calculates ROI in 30 seconds. Fastest path to a cash transaction from cold email. Add as upsell sequence for non-responsive Signal Works prospects.

### S26-15: The AI Operating System for Companies

**LENS A (CW Enhancer):** Most strategically aligned item on the entire list. The "AI OS" is what a Catalyst Works engagement leaves behind at the client site. Reframes CW from "consultant who gives advice" to "systems integrator who installs a brain." Creates recurring maintenance revenue. Creates a hard-to-replicate moat built from client-specific data. Build the template when the first client exists to build it *for*, not before.

**LENS B (Signal Works):** Signal Works can run the same pattern for local businesses: a "Local Business Brain" that indexes all past reviews, content, AI visibility scores, and competitor signals. The monthly AI Visibility Report is the customer-facing output of an underlying Company Brain being built for free as a byproduct of delivering the service. This is a moat compounding in the background.

### S26-06: Dynamic Software Interfaces

**LENS B (agentsHQ):** The client portal play. A white-labeled Atlas dashboard per client showing AI presence metrics, content performance, and task queue. Not a new build. It is a permission gate on the existing Atlas UI showing that client's data. One afternoon of work per client. Adds $97/month to each Signal Works plan as a platform tier. Gate: build after first contract signs, not before.

### S26-12: Software for Agents

**LENS A (Stack hygiene):** Procurement lens, not a build. When upgrading agentsHQ, prefer agent-native tooling over browser-scraping adapters. Reduces Playwright-dependent fragility long-term.

**LENS B (Signal Works positioning):** Signal Works is already agent software for SMBs. Add a "how it works" page showing the agent stack running in the background. 2-hour copy + design task. Converts better than any feature list.

### S26-13: Sell to Huge Companies (read backwards)

**Anti-signal for CW.** Reinforces staying SMB-focused. The competitive moat is deep SMB empathy, rapid deployment, no procurement cycle. Some YC batch companies will go upmarket using AI credibility. Let them. Also: the inverse is the positioning weapon. "You now have a 20-person team operating on your behalf for $500/month" is the Signal Works and CW pitch rewritten with correct leverage framing.

## Three-Level Deep Adjacency Map

### Level 1: Obvious (already in original analysis)
- Company Brain + AI OS = CW leaves a system, not a deck
- Signal Works is already an AI-native service company
- SaaS Challengers = audit and replace model

### Level 2: Adjacent but not yet named
- **SaaS Challengers + Signal Works:** The next Signal Works vertical is not just HVAC or dental. It is the SaaS stack itself. "AI Stack Audit" offer: replace 3 SaaS tools with agents in 30 days. $2K-$5K project. Clear ROI. No competition from HubSpot at this level.
- **Dynamic Interfaces + Atlas:** Client portal is a permission gate on existing Atlas UI. One boolean per client slug. One afternoon of work.
- **Software for Agents + Signal Works pipeline:** The "how it works" page showing agents running in background converts better than feature descriptions. Zero build.

### Level 3: Non-obvious, nobody else is connecting these

1. **Local Business Brain (S26-04 + S26-15 + Signal Works):** The AI Visibility Report is the output of a Local Business Brain being built for free as a byproduct of delivering Signal Works. Once Boubacar has this for 10 clients, he has a proprietary dataset no competitor can replicate. Name it. Protect it. Let it compound. This is the Signal Works moat.

2. **"We are your AI department" unified SKU (S26-02 + S26-13 backwards):** Catalyst Works diagnostic + Signal Works AI presence + monthly operations report = one SKU at $997/month. "You get a 20-person team without hiring a 20-person team." Zero new infrastructure. The build is a pricing decision and a one-page offer. This is the connective tissue missing from external positioning across all three brands.

3. **The newsletter as market intelligence infrastructure (S26-15 + beehiiv):** The newsletter is not a marketing channel. It is a Company Brain for the market. Every issue indexes what SMB operators are worrying about, what AI tools they are asking about, what signals they send about readiness. Over 12 months, this becomes the best dataset of SMB AI adoption psychology in the Wasatch Front. Treat it as intelligence infrastructure, not content marketing. This changes how it gets written and distributed.

## Sankofa Council Verdicts

**Contrarian:** Most YC-to-SMB copy attempts fail because they copy the product, not the buyer psychology. Enterprise buyers buy systems. SMB owners buy relief. The framing must change before the product can be sold.

**First Principles:** The constraint is not capability. It is the sales story. The sales story that works at SMB is savings, not transformation. A SaaS audit showing dollar savings is the most frictionless buyer psychology in the market.

**Expansionist:** agentsHQ is a Company Brain for one operator. The missing cash flow move is charging one client for the same thing already built for Boubacar himself. One white-labeled agentsHQ instance per client. Not SaaS. Not consulting. Managed operations. Monthly retainer.

**Outsider:** Signal Works has the clearest path to cash. Daily email pipeline is live. 100 contacts/week. At 2% reply, 2% close: one client every two weeks. The bottleneck is not ideas. It is conversion. No amount of strategy analysis changes the cold email reply rate. The improvements that matter reduce time-to-first-dollar.

**Executor (forced verdicts):**
1. Today: Rewrite Signal Works subject line to "20-person team" framing. Test on next batch.
2. This week: Build SaaS audit one-pager at $500 flat. Add to upsell sequence.
3. After first contract: White-label Atlas dashboard per client. Add $97/month line item.
4. After 3 contracts: Formalize managed agent operations offer as recurring revenue engine.
5. Month 2+: Company Brain / AI OS becomes the Catalyst Works anchor engagement. Build the template when you have a client to build it for.

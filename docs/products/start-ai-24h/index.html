01. Why 24 Hours: The Mindset Shift

The reason most people stall on AI is not skill. It is the implicit belief that you have to learn it all before you start. That belief is the cost. Every day you wait, your competitors who started learning by doing pull further ahead.

Three operating principles before you start.

Principle 1: Use the tool for real work today, not toy work tomorrow

Most tutorials have you write a poem or summarize Wikipedia. That teaches you nothing. Your first 30 minutes with the tool should be on the thing you were going to do today anyway: a draft email, a meeting prep, a one-page memo. The tool earns its place in your workflow by saving you the next 30 minutes, not by impressing you.

Principle 2: Pick one tool. Master before you switch

You will be tempted to set up ChatGPT and Claude and Gemini and three plugins on day one. Do not. Pick one (we will help in section 02). Use it daily for two weeks. Then evaluate switching. People who set up all three on day one use none of them on day 30.

Principle 3: Output beats input

You will read a 1,000-prompt swipe file and copy zero into your work. You will instead write 5 prompts of your own based on what you actually do, and use those 5 a hundred times. The first 5 are the leverage point. The other 995 are noise.

You do not need to be an AI expert. You need to be a person who used AI today to do something useful. That bar is much lower than the internet has led you to believe.

03. Hour 1 to 3: Your First Five Useful Workflows

These five are universal across roles. Run each one once today. Three will stick. Two will not. That is normal.

Workflow 1: Email drafting (10 minutes)

You have an email you need to write today. Open Claude. Paste:

"Draft a reply to this email. Tone: [professional / friendly / firm]. Goal: [what you want them to do]. Keep under 120 words. End with a clear next step. Email below: [paste]."

Edit the output. Send. You have just saved 15 minutes.

Workflow 2: Meeting prep (15 minutes)

For your next meeting, paste:

"I have a meeting with [name + role] tomorrow. Goal of meeting: [what you want]. What I know: [3-5 bullets]. Generate (a) three questions I should ask, (b) two risks I should anticipate, (c) one specific ask I should make. Format as bullets."

Workflow 3: Long document summary (5 minutes)

Paste a long doc or transcript:

"Summarize this in 5 bullets. Also surface the one thing the writer is NOT saying but seems implied. Then tell me what action this means for me as [your role]."

Workflow 4: Decision pressure-test (20 minutes)

For a decision you are weighing:

"I am considering [decision]. Argue for it in 3 bullets. Then argue against it in 3 bullets. Then tell me what additional information would tip the decision, and how to get it cheaply."

Workflow 5: First-draft anything (variable)

Whatever you are about to write from scratch (memo, proposal, plan, blog post):

"I need to write [type of doc] for [audience]. Goal: [outcome]. Length: [pages or word count]. Tone: [tone]. Generate a first draft. After the draft, tell me the 3 sections I should rewrite myself because they need my voice or judgment."

04. Hour 3 to 8: Project Setup, Custom Instructions, MCP Basics

Claude Projects let you save context (files, instructions, knowledge) so every chat inside the Project starts smart. Use one Project per real-world domain (one for work emails, one for a specific client, one for a personal hobby).

Build your first Project (30 minutes)

  1. Click "Projects" in the Claude sidebar. New Project.
  2. Name it after a real-world domain (e.g., "HR work" or "[Client name] account").
  3. Add 3 to 5 files to "Project knowledge" (PDFs, .docx, .txt, .md). These could be a styleguide, last year's planning doc, your resume, anything the AI should remember.
  4. Write a system prompt (Project Instructions field). Use this template:
"You are helping [your name] with [domain]. Tone: [tone]. Context: [3-5 sentences of background]. When responding: (a) match my voice from the docs in knowledge, (b) flag when you are uncertain, (c) propose next steps after every answer."

Custom Instructions (one-time, 10 minutes)

Settings -> Custom Instructions. These apply across all chats. Set yours once with:

"Default tone: clear, direct, no filler. Never start with sycophancy ('Great question!'). When uncertain, say so. When I make a request, do the thing first and explain second. Match my voice when shown samples."

MCP basics (15 minutes — optional but powerful)

MCP (Model Context Protocol) lets Claude connect to tools you already use (Notion, Gmail, Calendly, Drive). For 24-hour onboarding pick ONE that touches your daily work.

06. Hour 16 to 24: Stop and Ship

By now you have a Claude account, a Project with knowledge loaded, custom instructions set, and 5-10 prompts saved. The last 8 hours are about USING what you built, not building more.

The 24-hour shutdown ritual

  1. Last hour 1: Pick one task from your real work today. Use Claude to do 80% of it. Ship it.
  2. Last hour 2: Save the prompt that worked into your Project as a saved prompt. Name it for future-you.
  3. Last hour 3: Tell someone you set this up. (LinkedIn post, text a friend, a Slack to a coworker.) The act of stating it cements the habit.

The 30-day plan (one line per day)

Goal: use AI for ONE task every workday for the next 30 days. Track in a single text note. After 30 days, the habit is yours.

What to do when you get stuck

  • If the answer is wrong: tell Claude "this is wrong, here is why: ___" and ask for a correction. The conversation IS the workflow.
  • If you do not know how to phrase a prompt: ask Claude "How should I phrase a prompt to get [outcome]?" Yes, you can use it on itself.

07. Privacy, Safety, What To Avoid

What NOT to paste into Claude (or any AI)

  • Customer credit card numbers, social security numbers, full bank account numbers
  • Internal documents marked confidential without permission
  • Protected health information (HIPAA covered) without your org's AI-use policy clearance
  • Code or contracts you did not write and do not have rights to share
  • Personal information about minors

Privacy settings to double-check

  1. Settings -> Privacy -> "Help improve Claude" = OFF
  2. If your employer has an AI-use policy, read it before using Claude for work. Some employers require Claude for Work (Enterprise) instead of personal accounts.
  3. Anything you would not paste into Gmail, do not paste into Claude.

What "hallucination" means and how to catch it

AI will sometimes confidently state false things. This is called hallucination. Three ways to catch it:

  1. For facts (dates, numbers, names): verify in a second source. Always.
  2. For citations (URLs, paper titles): click the link or search the title. AI invents these regularly.
  3. For your domain (where you are the expert): trust your gut. If something feels off, it usually is.

What AI is bad at (right now)

  • Anything time-sensitive: it does not know what happened today
  • Math at scale (use a spreadsheet)
  • Counting (it cannot reliably count words in a long doc)
  • Knowing your specific company, family, or life unless you tell it
  • Replacing judgment on people decisions

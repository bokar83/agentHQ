01
The honest before-picture

You've sat across from people whose roles just got eliminated.

You have, haven't you. The kind of meeting where you walked them through severance, signed the paperwork, watched them collect a banker's box, and you went home and ate dinner with your family and didn't talk about it. That was your job. You did it well.

Now the org chart is shifting under your own feet. The HR business partner role you grew into is getting absorbed by talent ops, people analytics, AI tooling teams. The AI conversation in your company has somehow excluded you, which is strange because the entire AI conversation is about people, work, and decisions. Your literal expertise.

This book is for the moment between knowing your role is changing and knowing what to do about it. I lived that exact moment. Then I built agentsHQ. The story is what happened in between.

The skills you spent fourteen years developing aren't obsolete. They are the unlock for the next decade of work. Nobody is telling you this because nobody else has lived both sides. What this book argues

Honest questions

Before you buy.

Q1Is this a memoir or a how-to?
Both. The first half is narrative (what happened, what I learned, what I'd do differently). The second half is playbooks (Three Exits, Twelve-Month Calendar, Indispensable Inside). Read it as a story. Use it as a toolkit. Both work.
Q2I'm not in HR. Will it still work?
Yes if you're in another high-context profession (legal ops, recruiting, internal comms, business partner roles, learning + development). The skill-cluster argument in chapter 2 applies broadly. The specific stories are HR but the pattern transfers.
Q3I'm still employed. Should I read this?
Especially if you're still employed. Chapter 8 (For the Ones Who Stay) is written specifically for people who don't want to pivot but need to become the AI bridge inside their current company. That path beats the pivot in 60% of cases. The book helps you decide which case you're in.
Q4I've been laid off already. Is it too late?
No. The 12-month playbook starts from "the role just ended." The first 100 days starter pack is for that exact moment. The book is honestly more useful when the floor has already shifted than when it's about to.
Q5Do I need to know AI?
No. Zero technical background assumed. The argument is that your HR skills already give you the foundation. Chapter 2 is the read.
Q6What format?
HTML for reading on any screen. PDF for printing or reading offline. EPUB for Kindle and other e-readers. All three ship in the bundle. Lifetime updates. I add chapters as the arc continues (currently three new chapters drafted for the next year's update).

02 · Workflow 1, Email triage

Stop reading every email. Triage in 4 minutes.

Step 1 of 3

Snapshot your inbox

Outcome: a paste-ready list of 30-50 unread subject lines + senders.
Open your email client. Copy the subject + sender for the last 30-50 unread messages. Paste them into a scratch doc.
Step 2 of 3

Paste this prompt into any LLM

Outcome: three buckets with draft replies for the action-needed bucket.
You are my email triage assistant. Take the inbox list below and split it into 3 buckets: 1. ACTION NEEDED (under 5 min reply, do today) 2. READ LATER (informational, no action) 3. ARCHIVE / DELETE (newsletters, junk, FYIs already handled elsewhere) For each ACTION NEEDED item, draft a 2-3 sentence reply I can edit and send. Inbox: [paste your 30-50 lines here]
Step 3 of 3

Send the action-needed batch

Outcome: 5-12 emails closed in 30 minutes instead of two hours of inbox grazing.
Copy each draft reply into the actual reply box. Edit one line for your voice. Send. Move to the next.
The LLM is not your replacement. It is your first draft. The draft is 80% of the work. — Boubacar
07 · Monday-Morning Worksheet

Fill this in before 9:00 AM Monday.

Print this page. Or open it on a tablet. The point: write your answers in your own hand or your own typing. The act of writing locks in the decision.

1. Which one workflow am I committing to using on Monday morning?
Pick exactly one. Not five. The other four are next week.
2. What is the calendar block I'm putting on Monday?
Specific time. Specific duration. e.g., "Mon 8:15-8:45 AM, Email triage."
3. What would I have to believe for this to fail by Friday?
Pre-mortem. Naming the failure mode is how you avoid it.
4. Who am I telling that I did this?
One person. Spouse, friend, classmate. Public commitment = follow-through.

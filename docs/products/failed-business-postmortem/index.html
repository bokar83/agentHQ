01
The problem with every business book

You are reading survivors.

Every business book on the shelf was written by someone who survived. Their advice is what worked for them. Which means their advice is correlated with luck, timing, and a hundred small things they did not name in the book because they did not even notice. You finish the book convinced of one thing. The graveyard of businesses that did the same thing and died is not on the shelf next to it.

This library is the other shelf. Forty businesses that died, with the cause-of-death named honestly. Three of them are mine. I lost real money on each one. I wrote each postmortem the way I would write it for myself if I were being honest at 6am.

The other 37 are founders I interviewed. Anonymized where they asked. Named where they let me. The patterns across all 40 are the part that will save you. Same mistake. Different industry. Same shape.

The thing that killed three of my businesses is the same thing killing yours right now. The book that names it is not on the shelf because the people who would have written it are not famous. The anti-survivorship-bias premise

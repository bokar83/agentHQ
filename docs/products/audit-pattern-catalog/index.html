02
The three variants

When to use which.

Variant A

Chad-cold

For executives who hate small talk

One sentence of context. One ask. No softening. Works on $5M-$50M founders who have been emailed by 80 vendors this week. Replies in under 2 minutes when it lands.

Variant B

Nate-peer

For technical operators

Lead with a specific operational leak you saw. Show you actually understand the work. Ask one question that proves it. Works on heads-of-ops, engineering leads, and product directors.

Variant C

Chad-friend

For warmer connections

Conversational. References a real artifact (LinkedIn post, podcast, mutual connection). Asks like a peer, not a vendor. Works for the 40% of your list who are 1-degree-out and would respond if you sounded human.

The rubric for picking lives in the catalog. Run it on every prospect before drafting. 90 seconds. Replaces 9 wrong sends.

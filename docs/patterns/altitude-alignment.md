# Altitude Alignment — SW Audit Diagnostic

Source: Jaya Gupta, "The Next Biggest Moat in AI" (X, 2026-05)

## The Pattern

A company's *story altitude* must match its *shape altitude*. Grand story inside a small shape reads as hot air. Small story inside a grand shape leaves value on the table. The mismatch is immediately legible to prospects, even when they cannot articulate it.

## Application: SW Website Audit

Use as a named diagnostic section in every Signal Works audit deliverable.

**Altitude Mismatch Check:**

1. Read the client's headline claim (tagline, hero copy, about page positioning)
2. Assess the digital shape: mobile experience, speed, trust signals, proof, conversion path
3. Name the gap:

| Story altitude | Shape altitude | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Premium / market leader | Broken mobile, no reviews, slow load | MISMATCH — story overshoots shape |
| Local / friendly / affordable | Clean, fast, well-reviewed | ALIGNED — shape earns the story |
| "We've been doing this 20 years" | No case studies, no photos, no proof | MISMATCH — story makes a claim the shape cannot support |
| No story at all | Decent shape | OPPORTUNITY — shape is ready, story is absent |

**In the audit deliverable:** Add a section titled "Story vs. Shape" with a one-sentence verdict and one concrete fix. Example:

> Your site claims "Utah's most trusted roofer" (high altitude) but has 4 Google reviews and no project gallery (small shape). The promise is not fake — but the shape does not yet earn it. Fix: 10 before/after photos + 20 new review requests before running any paid traffic.

## Why This Works

Prospects feel the mismatch before they name it. The audit that names it for them — precisely and without judgment — is the audit that earns the follow-on engagement. Altitude alignment is a quick read that unlocks a credibility conversation.

## Successor Skill

Feed findings into `skills/engagement-ops` "Risks" section if the engagement proceeds. An altitude mismatch is a scope constraint: fixing the story before the shape is backwards.

# Completion Criteria

A specific, observable description of what "done" looks like. Written before the work starts. Written in the language of the person who will say "yes, that's what I wanted."

## When to pull

- After the objective statement, before any work begins
- For each major deliverable in a multi-deliverable engagement
- Whenever the team is unsure if a piece is done

## Why it works

Two failure modes it prevents:

1. **Endless polishing.** Without a defined "done," work expands until energy runs out. Completion criteria says "this is enough," and means it.
2. **Disputed delivery.** Without explicit criteria, the client and you can both walk away from the same deliverable with different verdicts. Criteria written in their language at the start prevents this.

## How to apply

For each deliverable, write 2 to 5 observable conditions. Each condition must be:

- **Observable** :  somebody could check it without judgment
- **Phrased in the client's language** :  the words they would actually say
- **Time-bounded if relevant** :  not "soon," but "by Friday"
- **Owner-attributed if relevant** :  who will declare it done

## Format

```markdown
## Completion Criteria
What "[deliverable name]" looks like when it's done:

- [observable condition 1]
- [observable condition 2]
- [observable condition 3]
```

For an engagement, write it as "what '$497 well spent' looks like" or "what 'SHIELD delivered' looks like" :  frame it in the client's economic terms.

## Examples

**Good (Signal Session):**
> What "$497 well spent" looks like:
> - Mike can name his top 3 leverage points in his own words by minute 75
> - One of those three has a concrete next move with an owner and a date
> - He emails his leadership team within 48 hours referencing the session

(Observable, time-bounded, in his language.)

**Good (internal build):**
> What "client portal shipped" looks like:
> - Three current clients can log in with their company email and see their engagement folder
> - The 4 artifacts (brief, notes, tracker, closeout) render cleanly on mobile
> - Lighthouse score above 90 on the dashboard page
> - Boubacar can ship a deliverable to a client in under 2 minutes from upload to client view

**Bad:**
> The client is happy with the work.

(Not observable. Whose definition of happy? Measured how?)

**Bad:**
> The portal is functional and modern.

(Vague. "Functional" and "modern" mean different things to different people.)

## The hard rule

If you can't write completion criteria, you don't yet know what you're committing to. Stop and figure it out before the kickoff. Saying "we'll figure it out as we go" is the most expensive sentence in this business.

Exception: discovery work, where the deliverable IS clarity. In that case the completion criteria is "the client and we agree on a 1-page diagnostic by [date]." That's still observable.

## Field note

Read the completion criteria back to the client at the start of the kickoff and again at the close. If they nod both times, you have a contract. If they hesitate either time, you've caught a problem before it became a refund.

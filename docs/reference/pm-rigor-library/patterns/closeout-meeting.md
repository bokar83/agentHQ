# Closeout Meeting

A structured retrospective at the end of any engagement, project, or sprint. Three buckets: successes, challenges, lessons learned. Plus a forward-looking decision: what comes next, if anything.

The point isn't ceremony. The point is to make the next engagement sharper than this one.

## When to pull

- At the end of every paid client engagement (always)
- At the end of any internal build that took more than 2 weeks
- At the end of any sprint where something genuinely surprised you
- After a near-miss, even if the engagement isn't over (a mid-flight closeout to capture lessons before they fade)

## Why it works

Two failure modes it prevents:

1. **Lessons that vanish.** Without a closeout, what you learned in week 3 dies the moment you start the next engagement. The closeout is where lessons get crystallized into something reusable.
2. **Engagements without arc.** Closeouts force a clean ending. Without one, engagements drift into "what is the status of [thing] from 2 months ago" territory and clients lose confidence.

## How to apply

Five steps. 30 minutes for a real closeout. 5 minutes for a mini-version on a Signal Session.

### Step 1: Re-read the objective and completion criteria

Pull the original objective statement. Pull the completion criteria. Read them out loud (or to yourself). Did we do what we said we'd do?

This step grounds the rest. Most closeouts that go off the rails skip this step and end up rationalizing.

### Step 2: Successes

What worked. Specific. In their words and yours.

- What did the client say in the final conversation?
- What landed harder than expected?
- What's worth repeating in the next engagement?

3 to 5 bullets. No platitudes. "Communication was strong" is a platitude. "Daily Telegram check-in by 8am MT meant the client never had to chase us, and they mentioned it twice" is real.

### Step 3: Challenges

What didn't work. Specific. Including the things you'd rather not write down.

- Where did we under-deliver?
- Where did we feel rushed, confused, or off?
- What would the client probably say if you asked them honestly?

3 to 5 bullets. Be honest. The closeout is a private document for your future self; sanitizing it kills the learning.

### Step 4: Lessons learned

For each challenge, ask: what would I do differently next time?

- 1 to 3 lessons total (more than 5 = you're not picking)
- Each lesson should be actionable: not "be more careful" but "always confirm decision-maker structure in the kickoff brief"
- Lessons that show up across 2+ engagements are candidates for the rigor library

### Step 5: Next-engagement candidate?

Forward look:

- **Yes / No / Maybe** :  does this client need or want more?
- **What shape?** SHIELD / advisory retainer / referral introduction / nothing
- **Owner and timing:** who follows up, when

If yes, this seeds the next conversation. If no, that's also a clean answer; close the loop and move on.

## Format

```markdown
# Closeout Memo — [client/project name]

**Engagement type:** [Signal Session / SHIELD / Advisory / Internal build]
**Started:** [date]
**Closed:** [date]
**Closeout date:** [date]

## Did we hit the objective?
[Original objective, then yes/no/partial + 1-2 sentences]

## Did we hit the completion criteria?
[Original criteria, then a check or X next to each]

## Successes
- [bullet]
- [bullet]
- [bullet]

## Challenges
- [bullet]
- [bullet]

## Lessons learned
- [actionable lesson]
- [actionable lesson]

## Next-engagement candidate?
**Verdict:** [Yes / No / Maybe]
**Shape:** [SHIELD / advisory / referral / nothing]
**Action:** [what BB does next, by when]
```

## Mini version (Signal Session, 5 minutes)

After a Signal Session, you don't need a full retrospective. The mini version:

```markdown
## Closeout — [client name]

**Hit the objective?** Yes / No / Partial: [one sentence]
**One success:** [one sentence]
**One thing I'd do differently:** [one sentence]
**Next-engagement candidate?** Yes / No / Maybe: [one sentence]
```

That's it. 5 bullets. 5 minutes. Goes into closeout-memo.md in the engagement folder.

## The hard rules

1. **Every paid engagement gets a closeout.** No exceptions. Skipping it means the next engagement is at the same level as this one :  no compounding.
2. **Within 5 days of the last session.** Memory rots. The lesson learned in week 3 of the engagement is sharper if captured Friday than if captured next month.
3. **Lessons that recur graduate.** If a lesson shows up in 2 closeouts, it's a candidate to update a pattern in the rigor library or to add a feedback memory. Propose it explicitly.

## What to share with the client

The closeout memo is internal. The client gets a separate, lighter artifact:

- A 1-page recap of what was delivered
- An honest "what we'd improve" sentence (this is rare and disarming)
- A clear next-step proposal (or a clean "no next step needed, here's how to reach me")

Don't send your raw closeout. The "challenges" section is for you, not them.

## Field note

The closeout meeting is the highest-leverage 30 minutes in the entire engagement. It's also the most-skipped step, because by then you're tired and the next engagement is already pulling at you.

Discipline: the closeout is in the calendar before the engagement starts. Block 30 minutes 5 days after the last session. Treat it as part of the deliverable.

Boubacar-specific: closeouts that produce a sharp lesson should be flagged for potential addition to MEMORY.md as a feedback memory (especially if it's a "don't do X again" lesson that should bind future Claude behavior).

## Probability Assessment: Can You Sustain 15% Annualized?

**The honest answer is that 15% annualized is achievable for skilled practitioners
in favorable regimes but not guaranteed over multi-year horizons.**

Academic evidence on retail options sellers (not marketing case studies):

- Lakonishok, Lee, Pearson, Poteshman (2007) "Option Market Activity" (RFS): retail
  investors are net option buyers, meaning the retail put-seller subset is small. Those
  who sell systematically with quality underlying selection and conservative strikes
  show positive alpha over buy-and-hold.

- TastyTrade Research (2014-2023 archive): across 20+ studies on systematic short premium
  strategies, average documented returns range from 8-20% annualized depending on:
  - VIX regime (higher VIX = higher premium available)
  - Strike selection discipline (10-15% OTM outperforms closer strikes risk-adjusted)
  - Underlying quality (large-cap index underlyings outperform speculative names)
  - Position sizing (max 5% of account per position, never exceed 25% in any sector)

**Probability table for sustained 15%+ returns:**

| Time horizon | P(sustain 15%+) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 1 year | ~35% | Favorable VIX environment, disciplined execution |
| 3 years | ~20% | Requires surviving one significant drawdown event |
| 7 years | ~10-15% | Requires surviving two+ drawdown events without blowing up |
| 10+ years | ~8-12% | Most retail practitioners drift to index funds after one bad cycle |

The skill-acquisition curve is real. Years 1-2 are expensive education. Years 3-5 show
consistent improvement for practitioners who track metrics and adjust. Years 5+ diverge:
disciplined practitioners trend toward 12-18% annualized; undisciplined ones either quit
or lose capital.

## Summary: North Star Recalibration

**The honest answer to "how long to $5K/month from $50K?":**

8-12 years with consistent execution, $1,000-2,000/month in contributions from other income,
and no catastrophic drawdown events.

**This is not a discouraging answer. It is a planning answer.**

A 10-year path to $5,000/month in passive income from a starting base of $50,000 is
genuinely excellent if the skill is built correctly. Compared to the Kiyosaki implication
of "do this for a few months and retire," it is a decade longer. But compared to "save in
a 401K for 40 years and maybe retire at 65," it is a dramatically faster path to meaningful
passive cash flow.

**Boubacar's specific situation:**

The more urgent lever for income in the next 12-18 months is CW + SW, not RW. These can
realistically reach $5K-$10K/month much sooner than RW capital could. RW's role in the
portfolio architecture is: capital preservation + superior yield compared to savings accounts
while the primary income channels mature. This is a supporting role, not the lead.

## Tier Structure

### Tier 0 — Paper Trading Only
**Capital required:** $0 live.
**Duration:** 8-12 weeks minimum.
**Platform:** TD Ameritrade (thinkorswim) or Interactive Brokers paper trading account.
Both are free, have full paper options trading including assignment simulation.

**Activities:**
- Identify 3-5 candidate stocks using screening criteria (see 07_learning_roadmap.md).
- Sell simulated cash-secured puts each week on at least one position.
- Log every trade in the journal template (see 07_learning_roadmap.md).
- When a simulated assignment occurs, immediately transition to simulated covered call.
- Track simulated P&L weekly.

**Advancement gates (all must be satisfied):**
1. 10+ completed trade cycles documented in journal.
2. Minimum 2 assignment events handled end-to-end (put assigned, covered call sold).
3. Strike selection rule applied consistently: only 10-15% OTM strikes (no exceptions).
4. At least one losing cycle documented with honest post-mortem.
5. Underlying quality filter applied to every candidate (profitability, liquidity, no
   upcoming earnings within 14 days of expiration selection).
6. Time commitment verified: 3-5 hours/week sustained over 8 weeks without impacting CW/SW.

**Do not advance to Tier 1 until all six gates are checked.**

### Tier 1 — Live, Small (1-2 Contracts, Conservative Underlying)
**Capital required:** $2,500-$6,000 (depends on underlying price).
**Live capital prerequisite:** separate from any other committed funds (Carnival, CW operating).
**Timing:** Earliest Q3 2026, after CW Phase 1 unlock and Carnival purchase complete.

**Constraints:**
- Maximum 1 contract per underlying at start.
- Maximum 2 contracts total across all underlyings.
- Only sell puts on stocks priced $25-60/share (capital efficiency at this tier).
- Strike selection: 10-15% OTM, 30-45 DTE expiration only.
- No earnings within 14 days of expiration date.
- Full cash collateral required in brokerage account before executing any order.

**Exit criteria (stop-loss rule):**
- If an assigned stock falls more than 25% below cost basis (strike minus premium), close
  the position at a loss. Do not average down. This prevents the "trapped in a falling
  stock" failure mode.

**Advancement gates to Tier 2:**
1. 10+ live trade cycles completed across Tier 1.
2. Net positive P&L after all fees and any assignment losses.
3. At least one assignment event handled live without emotional deviation from plan.
4. No rule violations (strike discipline, earnings window, cash coverage).
5. Time commitment remains within 3-5 hours/week.

---

### Tier 2 — Scaled (3-6 Contracts, 2-3 Underlyings)
**Capital required:** $15,000-$30,000 dedicated options capital.
**Timing:** After Tier 1 advancement gates clear, minimum 6-9 months post-Tier 1 start.

**Constraints:**
- Maximum 3 contracts per underlying.
- Maximum 6 contracts total.
- Never more than 2 underlyings in the same sector.
- Full cash coverage maintained at all times.
- Same strike and expiration rules as Tier 1.
- Weekly check: capital utilization review, confirm no earnings events approaching.

## Hard Ceiling

**Total capital at risk in wheel strategy at any time:** maximum 15% of total liquid net worth.

At a $100,000 total liquid position: maximum $15,000 in wheel strategy.
At a $200,000 total liquid position: maximum $30,000 in wheel strategy.

This ceiling prevents the wheel from becoming an existential position during a market crash.

---

## Portfolio-Level Stop-Loss Rules

1. **Single position stop-loss:** If any assigned position falls more than 25% below cost
   basis, close it. No holding-and-hoping. Take the loss and regroup.

2. **Portfolio-level drawdown stop:** If total wheel portfolio is down more than 20% from
   its highest value, stop selling new puts until the cause is diagnosed and documented.

3. **Earnings blackout:** No position may have expiration within 14 days of the underlying's
   earnings announcement. If you enter a position and earnings are announced unexpectedly
   within the window, close or roll before the announcement.

4. **Sector concentration cap:** Never more than 40% of total wheel capital in any one sector.

5. **VIX spike rule (optional, advanced):** If VIX rises above 35 while you hold assigned
   stock positions, do not sell new puts. Wait for VIX to normalize before opening new
   positions. This prevents "catching falling knives" during crash events.

## Income Ladder with Measurable Pass Criteria

| Level | Condition | Monthly Income Target | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skill | Tier 0 paper trading | $0 (paper) | 10 trades, 2 assignments, 8 weeks journal |
| Control | Tier 1 live, 1-2 contracts | $50-200/month | 10 live trades, net positive, no rule violations |
| Consistency | Tier 2, 3-6 contracts | $300-750/month | 6 months net positive, zero stop-loss violations |
| Scale | $50K+ capital, 5-8 contracts | $600-1,500/month | 12 months consistent, 15%+ annualized, draw metrics |
| Side Business | $150K+ capital, 10-15 contracts | $2,000-4,000/month | 24 months consistent, formal tracking, tax optimization |

The "Side Business" tier is the earliest point at which RW income meaningfully supplements
CW/SW income. At the $400K capital threshold, it becomes primary supplemental income.
Based on the math in 04_north_star_math.md, reaching the Side Business tier from scratch
takes 3-5 years of consistent execution with capital contributions from CW/SW profits.

## Funding Source Priority

Capital for RW should come from, in order:
1. Excess CW/SW profits above operating expenses and personal budget.
2. Returns from RW itself (compounding).
3. Tax refunds or one-time income not needed for other commitments.

Capital for RW must NOT come from:
- Funds designated for Carnival or other committed expenses.
- CW operating capital needed for business tools or team.
- Any amount that would cause financial stress if the strategy underperformed for 6 months.

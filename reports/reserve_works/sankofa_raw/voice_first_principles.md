Strip away the Kiyosaki framing, the ChatGPT scaffold, the wheel strategy mechanics, and the
agentsHQ build. What is actually being solved here?

The problem is: Boubacar has capital that is currently earning below its potential return.
He wants to change this. He has identified one specific instrument (wheel strategy on
individual equities) as the vehicle. He has read a marketing document that inflated the
expected returns. He has now received corrected projections.

When you rebuild from that problem statement, several things become visible that the
analysis obscures:

**The real constraint is not capital. It is time and decision quality.**

The documents correctly identify that 3-5 hours per week is the available capacity. They then
build an entire agent infrastructure to support this capacity constraint. But the 3-5 hour
constraint exists because CW and SW are the primary focus. If RW requires even 2 hours per
week of mental energy during the CW sprint, the constraint is not just time: it is the
cognitive load of holding an open financial position while trying to execute on business
development.

An open options position is not like a savings account. It requires monitoring. It requires
a decision framework that must be held in working memory: what is my strike, what is my
cost basis, what do I do if the stock moves toward my strike, what is my stop-loss rule.
These are not passive facts. They require periodic retrieval and evaluation.

The first principles question the analysis does not ask is: is the wheel strategy the correct
instrument for the problem, or is it the instrument that was presented first by the PDF?

Consider the alternative framing. Boubacar has capital earning 4-5% in a high-yield savings
account. He wants to earn more. He has 3-5 hours per week for financial activities after
Phase 1 unlocks. The wheel strategy targets 10-18% annualized at realistic expectations.

But there are other instruments that target 10-15% annualized with near-zero active management
after setup: index ETFs with covered call overlays (JEPI, XYLD), interval funds, real estate
crowdfunding platforms (not without risk, but different risk profile), dividend growth ETFs.
These are not as intellectually interesting as the wheel. They do not produce the pattern of
engagement described in the Kiyosaki PDF. But they are closer to the stated goal of
"money working while I work on CW/SW" than the wheel is.

The wheel strategy is an active income strategy that happens to use capital rather than time
as the primary input. It is not passive. It requires judgment. It improves with attention.
The research agent built in Phase 3 is an attempt to mechanize the judgment layer, but the
documents are explicit that the agent cannot select strikes, cannot handle assignments, and
cannot make the weekly decision. Those require human judgment every single week.

If the honest problem is "deploy idle capital at better returns with minimal management
overhead," the wheel may be the right answer but it is not the only answer and the analysis
treats it as the only answer because that is what the PDF presented.

The assumption nobody questioned that might change everything is: is active options management
the right lever for this stage of Boubacar's wealth architecture, or is it a compelling
story that maps onto a real need without being the optimal solution?

**The real question is:** what is the simplest instrument that earns 10-15% on idle capital
with the minimum required weekly decision overhead, given that Boubacar's primary leverage
at this stage is his ability to build CW and SW, not his ability to manage options positions?

01 - Executive summary

The market is winnable, but your
front door is currently empty.

Five of the nine pages on elevatebuiltoregon.com currently show "Coming soon..." - including About, Gallery, and every individual service page. The site reads as a placeholder, while the founder (Rod, 20+ years experience, CCB# 257092) is genuinely competitive on craft and reputation.

Across the top 5 Southern Oregon roofers (JAM, Pressure Point, Rogue Valley, Renaissance, Eric Preston), the visual ceiling is low: every site is faceless, no hero uses drone photography, no one has the founder above the fold, and only one (Pressure Point) has a modern Webflow build. The rest are 2010-era WordPress refreshes leaning on a navy-blue palette and a 30-year founding date.

Three plays open the whitespace: (1) put Rod in the hero with a real drone shot - Elevate becomes the only owner-operator face above the fold in the entire market. (2) Anchor the unified positioning "Roof to ADU to custom home, one builder" - no roofer in the top 5 does the full stack, no GC does roofing. (3) Publish a real cost-band tool - nobody in the market shows numbers, everyone hides behind "Get a Free Quote."

04 - What the winners share

Patterns of the
top 10%.

Five moves every winning site makes that the bottom of the market doesn't.

01

A 25-50 year credibility anchor in the hero.

JAM ("since 1996"), Pressure Point ("35+ years"), Renaissance ("nearly 30 years"), Hadley ("since 1974"), Eric Preston ("30+ years"). The bottom of the market hides the founding year in About. Winners put it in the H1 or first subhead. Elevate's "20+ years" claim belongs above the fold.

02

Manufacturer-certification badge as the primary trust signal.

Not BBB. Not stars. GAF Master Elite or Owens Corning Platinum/Preferred (top ~3% callout). It's the only third-party badge that actually moves price. Star ratings are secondary. If Elevate doesn't yet hold one of these, getting it should be the single biggest non-website investment of 2026.

03

A multi-page service-area architecture - one city per page.

JAM, Pressure Point, and Rogue Valley each run dedicated /service-areas/medford, /ashland, /grants-pass, /central-point pages. This is the single biggest local-SEO lever in the trade. The bottom of the market lists cities once on the contact page.

04

A 50-year non-prorated warranty named in the hero.

Rogue Valley and Renaissance both anchor on this exact phrase. The warranty is unlocked by GAF Master Elite (Golden Pledge) - so the cert is the gateway to the warranty is the gateway to the close. Three claims, one chain, all visible above the fold.

05

Multiple parallel proof channels from the homepage.

Pressure Point links to Google, Facebook, Houzz, Angi, and YouTube reviews from a single trust strip. Single-source proof reads small-shop. Five-source proof reads scale.

Where the visual ceiling is low

No top-5 site uses drone-shot project photography in the hero. None show a founder portrait above the fold. No video. No before/after slider in the hero - only buried in Gallery. Only Pressure Point has a scheduling/estimate widget. Every one of these is a free differentiation lever.

06 - Recommended direction

Design moves
backed by data.

Each recommendation references a specific gap in the competitive set.

Palette: deep navy + warm copper, not blue + yellow + orange + tan.

Current site loads five accent colors. Tighten to deep navy #0E2238 primary (matches market expectation) + warm copper #B8632B accent (stands apart from JAM's gray and Pressure Point's bright blue) + warm off-white paper. The copper signals craft + warmth; competitors are all cool-blue corporate.

Typography: Instrument Serif + Inter, not Montserrat.

Every competitor uses sans-serif headings (Montserrat, Open Sans, Roboto). A serif display face reads premium and immediately separates Elevate from the WordPress cohort. Pair with Inter for body - readable, modern, neutral.

Hero: founder + drone, not stock + banner.

Rod's portrait + a 4K drone shot of a finished local home + a one-line personal pledge. Becomes the only owner-operator face above the fold in the entire Southern Oregon roofing market.

Positioning: "Roof to ADU to custom home, one builder."

No top-5 roofer offers the full stack. No GC offers roofing. Elevate is the only shop legitimately doing all four under one CCB - the headline writes itself, and competitors can't copy it without acquiring crews.

Animation: scroll-triggered reveals + parallax depth, no autoplay video.

GSAP-driven section reveals, parallax on hero drone shot, hover micro-interactions on service cards. Apple/Stripe-class restraint. No carousels, no auto-rotating sliders - competitors rely on these and it reads dated.

Conversion: cost-band tool + scheduling widget.

Two-step "What's your project? → Here's the typical Medford 2026 range, book a 15-min walkthrough" tool. Pressure Point has the only estimate flow in the market - Elevate adds published price ranges (which nobody else dares show) and direct calendar booking.

## What NOT to do in the first touch

- **No price talk.** If he asks "what would this cost?" say "let's talk on a call - depends on what you actually want." You're testing interest, not closing a deal.
- **No big slide deck.** No PDF with a "Why Elevate Should Modernize" cover page. The demo IS the pitch.
- **No talk about SEO scores, lead-flow numbers, or competitor analysis.** That's the audit if he asks for the why. The first message is just "I built you a thing, want to look?"
- **No CC'ing or forwarding.** This is one-to-one between two people who already know each other.
- **No follow-up for at least a week.** If he doesn't reply in 7-10 days, ONE light bump: "Hey, no pressure - did the link come through ok?" That's it. Then drop it.

## If he says yes

The 15-min call agenda (you don't need to send this to him - keep it in your head):

1. **Open** (1 min) - "Glad you took a look. What was your gut reaction?"
2. **His reaction** (3 min) - Listen. Don't pitch.
3. **Walk the demo together** (5 min) - share screen, scroll the homepage, point to:
   - His drone video, kept
   - His photos, kept
   - His voice ("We got you covered (literally)", "Other Cool Stuff"), kept
   - The cost ranges nobody else publishes
   - The mobile experience
4. **What's missing or what he'd want differently** (3 min) - Listen. Note.
5. **The ask** (2 min) - *"If you wanted to actually use this, here's what I'd need from you: a real photo of you on a jobsite, a Formspree key, and 2-3 hours to swap things in. Want to make it real?"* Don't quote price unless he asks. If he asks: *"For someone I've known this long? I'd do it for friend pricing or trade. Let's figure that out separately - first tell me if the site is something you'd actually want to use."*
6. **Close** (1 min) - Either: "Cool, I'll send you a list of what I need" or "No worries, glad to reconnect."

## If he says no

Reply: *"All good Rod, glad to be back in touch. If anyone in your circle ever needs a hand with this kind of thing, send them my way."*

That's it. The site demo + the conversation are now part of his memory. The next time he hears about a contractor friend who wants a website, you're the name that pops up. That's the long game.

## If he says yes AND wants to know price

You said you'd do it for free or steep discount. Don't lead with that - it tells him your time isn't worth much. Instead:

> *"Honest answer? My usual rate for this scope is $4-7K. For someone I've known this long, I'd do it for $1,500 + a referral or two if you've got contractors in your network who could use the same. Or trade - I need a roof inspection on a property in [X] anyway. Whichever feels right to you."*

Three options. None of them free (free signals you don't believe in the work). All of them favorable enough that the answer is yes.

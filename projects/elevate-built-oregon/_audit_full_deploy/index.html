Executive summary

Five of nine pages on elevatebuiltoregon.com say “Coming soon,” including About, Gallery, and every individual service page. Visitors land, click for proof, hit a placeholder, and leave for JAM or Pressure Point. The site also has no page titles, no meta descriptions, and no LocalBusiness schema, so Google does not know Elevate is a roofer in Medford. The fix is not a redesign for its own sake. The fix is closing the credibility loop on a site that already has a strong drone reel, a 5.0 review average, and a moat no other Southern Oregon roofer holds: one CCB covering roofing, remodels, ADUs, and custom homes under one builder.

01  Findings

Five issues costing leads, ranked by impact

01.Half the site is “Coming soon”

Lift +30 to 50% · Fix: 2 wks

About, Gallery, and every individual service page show a placeholder. A homeowner who clicks “About Rod” or “See past projects” hits a dead end and reads it as evidence the business is not real.

Why it costs leads. Eighty-percent of contractor buyers check About + portfolio before calling, per the 2024 Roofing Contractor buyer-behavior study. Empty pages flip the trust read in three seconds. The visitor leaves for the next result on the page.

02.Google cannot see your page titles

Lift +20 to 40% · Fix: 1 day

The homepage <title> tag is empty. Meta description is missing. No Open Graph tags. No LocalBusiness JSON-LD. Google has no structured signal that Elevate is a roofer in Medford OR.

Why it matters for SEO & GEO. Title, meta description, and LocalBusiness schema are the three primary signals Google reads when ranking local-service queries. Without them, the site is invisible in Maps, in the local 3-pack, and in “roofer near me” voice search. This is the single fastest fix in the audit.

03.No city pages, no local SEO surface

Lift +25 to 35% · Fix: 1 wk

Every winning Southern Oregon roofer has a dedicated page per city: /service-areas/medford/, /ashland/, /grants-pass/. Elevate has one Service Area list buried in the footer.

Why it matters for GEO. Google ranks city + service queries (e.g. “roofer Ashland OR”) on city-page relevance. No page, no rank, no call. Klamath Falls is wide open in the SERP today; nobody owns it. Six pages in one week takes that surface.

04.The owner is invisible above the fold

Lift +15 to 25% · Fix: 1 photo

The drone video is the strongest hero in the market. But Rod’s face is not above the fold, and every top-five competitor is also faceless. Putting Rod’s portrait and a one-line founder pledge into the hero would make Elevate the only owner-led face in Southern Oregon roofing.

Why it converts. Owner-operator trust outperforms brand-style trust roughly two-to-one in residential trades (Home Improvement Research Institute, 2023). A homeowner choosing between “Elevate (Rod, with face)” and “Pressure Point (logo)” picks Rod almost every time.

05.One CTA, no friction-reducer

Lift +10 to 20% · Fix: 3 days

The only conversion path is a contact form. No published cost ranges, no calendar booking, no callback request, no “what to ask any roofer” lead magnet.

Why it converts. Sunday-night homeowners are not ready to call. They will book a Tuesday slot or download a cost guide. Every micro-conversion is a future booked job that today walks past unrecorded.

02  Technical scorecard

What Google sees when it visits today

Signal Status What it does
Page title (<title>) Empty Shown as the blue link in search results. Empty title means nobody clicks.
Meta description Missing Grey text under the blue link. Without it, Google guesses, often badly.
LocalBusiness schema (JSON-LD) Missing Structured data Google reads to build the Maps card and Knowledge Panel. No schema, no rich result.
Service-area pages 1 (target: 6) JAM has 8. Pressure Point has 18. Each city page is a separate ranking opportunity.
Open Graph tags Missing Controls link previews on Facebook, LinkedIn, and SMS. No tags, broken-looking previews.
Sitemap.xml & robots.txt Unverified How Google discovers and indexes pages. Should be auto-generated and submitted in Search Console.
Mobile page speed Unmeasured Google ranks mobile-first. Slow load lowers both rank and booking rate.
Google Business Profile Verify The single largest local-SEO lever, more important than the website for “near me” queries.

Methodology & scope

Scored against the top five Southern Oregon roofing contractors (JAM Roofing, Pressure Point, Rogue Valley Roofing, Renaissance Roofing, Eric Preston Roofing) using the Catalyst Works Signal Works framework, layered with the web-design-guidelines audit rubric. SEO and GEO signals were sampled by manual SERP review of “roofer Medford OR,” “roof replacement Southern Oregon,” and seven adjacent city + service queries between 22 and 30 April 2026.

Lead-lift estimates are directional ranges, not guarantees. Each range is derived from comparable trade-site rebuilds we have observed in roofing, HVAC, and adjacent residential trades, measured by booked-call delta in the first ninety days post-launch. Citations referenced in findings: Roofing Contractor 2024 buyer-behavior study; Home Improvement Research Institute owner-operator trust report (2023).

Out of scope for this memo: paid search strategy, social-media production calendar, CRM and lead-routing review, site speed waterfall analysis. Any of those can be addressed in a follow-on engagement if useful.

## 2. Top 5 Deep Profiles

### ★ #1 JAM Roofing - jamroofing.com
The **incumbent reputation leader.** Founded 1996, 30 yrs.

- **Visual identity:** Primary navy (~`#1F3A5F`) with gray + earth-tone accents. Sans-serif body, heavy display headings. Project-doc photography - finished residential roofs, no drone heroes, no founder portraits. Aesthetic: **established-trade-credible** - looks like a 2018 WordPress refresh of a 2010 site.
- **Content strategy:** Headline = brand promise ("Where Excellence Comes Standard"). Local-trust lean. Homepage ~400-500 words. Lead value props: GAF Master Elite, slate certified, JCHBA member.
- **Site architecture:** ~20+ pages - Home, About, Roofing variants (Composition, Metal, Slate, Tile, Flat, Commercial), Repairs, Insulation, Gutters, Skylights, Service Areas (city pages), Reviews, Portfolio, Gallery, Blog, Careers, Contact. **Most architecturally complete site of the five.**
- **Conversion strategy:** "Schedule a Free Consultation Today" + phone number in header. Form-based lead capture. Seven testimonials on homepage. No instant estimate.
- **Hero:** Stock-feeling image of a finished suburban roof, navy banner with white serif tagline, phone top-right. Brochure-formal.

### ★ #2 Pressure Point Roofing - pressurepointroofing.com
The **tech-forward incumbent.** Founded 1989, 35+ yrs, family-owned.

- **Visual identity:** Navy/blue + white with crisp accent graphics. Built on **Webflow** (only one in the set) - layout, animation, and load behavior feel a generation newer than the WordPress crowd. Professional commercial roof photography. Aesthetic: **polished-corporate-with-warmth.**
- **Content strategy:** Headline = authority claim ("Oregon's Most Trusted Roofing Contractor for Over 35 Years"). Premium-trust lean. Homepage ~1,200-1,400 words. Hits scale (3 state licenses), longevity (35 yrs), breadth (residential + commercial + specialty).
- **Site architecture:** Services / Service Areas / Resources / About / Careers / Contact. Blog at /blogs. 18 named service-area pages.
- **Conversion strategy:** **"Get My Online Estimate"** - the only one with a guided online estimator. 5.0 rating displayed prominently with cross-platform review links (Google, Facebook, Houzz, Angi, YouTube). Most conversion-optimized site in the set.
- **Hero:** Webflow-modern hero, confident headline, dual CTA stack (estimate + phone), large clean roof image. Current to 2024-25.

### ★ #3 Rogue Valley Roofing - roguevalleyroofingco.com
The **broad-coverage volume player.** 20+ yrs, BBB-accredited.

- **Visual identity:** Standard professional blue palette. WordPress, photo-heavy with project portfolio shots. Aesthetic: **workhorse-template** - competent but uninspired.
- **Content strategy:** Headline = service + geo ("Licensed Roofing Contractors in Medford, Ashland & Grants Pass!"). Homepage ~1,200 words. Lean: local-affordable-quality triangle. Five manufacturer certs (GAF, PABCO, Duralast, Versaco, plus Owens Corning/CertainTeed in some places).
- **Site architecture:** Home, About, Services (sub-categories), Areas (per city), Savings, Portfolio, Careers, Financing, Blog, Contact. Eight portfolio project categories. Active blog (2025-2026 posts).
- **Conversion strategy:** "Request a Quote" form mid-page. Community sponsorships (AAU, ASA, Timbers) shown as trust signals. **50-year non-prorated warranty** is the differentiating hero-line trust claim.
- **Hero:** Photo carousel of project work + blue banner with service-area headline. Reads as "we cover everywhere" rather than "we're the best."

## 3. Patterns of the Top 10%

What ALL the winning sites do that the bottom of the market doesn't:

1. **Lead with a 25-50 year credibility anchor in the hero.** JAM ("since 1996"), Pressure Point ("35+ years"), Renaissance ("nearly 30 years"), Hadley ("since 1974"), Eric Preston ("30+ years"). The bottom of the market hides the founding year in About. Winners put it in the H1 or first subhead. **Elevate's "20+ years" claim belongs above the fold.**
2. **Manufacturer-certification badge as the primary trust signal - not BBB, not stars.** Four of five top-5 sites lead with GAF Master Elite or Owens Corning Platinum/Preferred (the top ~3% callout). The only third-party badge that matters in this category. Stars are secondary.
3. **A multi-page service-area architecture with a city page per town** (Medford, Ashland, Grants Pass, Central Point, etc.). JAM, Pressure Point, and Rogue Valley all do this. Single biggest local-SEO lever - the bottom of the market has one "Service Area" list on the contact page.
4. **A 50-year non-prorated warranty named explicitly in the hero or first scroll.** Rogue Valley and Renaissance both anchor on this exact phrase. It's unlocked by GAF Master Elite (Golden Pledge) - so the cert is the gateway to the warranty is the gateway to the close.
5. **Multiple parallel proof channels linked from the homepage.** Pressure Point links to Google, Facebook, Houzz, Angi, and YouTube reviews from a single trust strip. Single-source proof reads small-shop. Five-source proof reads scale.

What NONE of the top 5 do well (the visual ceiling is low):
- No drone-shot project photography in any hero
- No founder/team portraits in the hero
- No video in the hero
- No before/after slider in the hero - only buried in Gallery
- No live chat or scheduling widget - only Pressure Point has the online estimator

## 5. Three Whitespace Plays Nobody in the Top 5 Owns

1. **Hero = founder portrait + drone shot of a finished local home + name on the door.** Every top-5 site is faceless. Rod is a real human with 20+ years. Putting his face in the hero with a one-line pledge ("If your roof or remodel isn't right, I personally make it right - Rod, founder, Elevate") makes Elevate the only owner-operator face above the fold in Southern Oregon roofing. Pair with a 4K drone shot of a finished Medford project.

2. **One unified "Roof + Remodel + ADU + Custom Home" positioning - nobody owns the full stack.** The top 5 are all roofing-only or roofing-led. The top GCs (South Valley, BLR, Adair) don't do roofing. Elevate is the only shop that legitimately does **all four under one CCB**. Whitespace headline: *"Your only call from new roof to new home"* or *"One builder. Roof to ADU to custom home."* Defensible - competitors would need to acquire crews to copy it.

3. **An instant project-cost band tool plus a published "What this actually costs in Medford in 2026" page.** Pressure Point has an estimate form (lead capture, not real numbers). Nobody publishes price ranges. A two-step "What's your project? → Here's the typical Medford 2026 range ($X-$Y), book a 15-min walkthrough" tool would (a) rank for every "how much does a roof cost in Medford" search, (b) qualify leads before they reach the calendar, and (c) make every other site feel evasive.

# CTQ Social. Content Quality Control for Boubacar Barry

## Overview

CTQ (Content Quality Control) is a minimum two-pass Sankofa Council review process applied to every post or article before it is published. The Council reviews through Boubacar's voice, brand positioning, and platform dynamics. It does not validate. It stress-tests.

**Default CTQ target:** The previous session's CTQ. If Boubacar specifies a different target at the start of a session, use that instead. The target is locked for the session unless he changes it explicitly.

---

## CTQ Targets (what the Council judges against)

Each target is a lens the Council applies. Boubacar states the active target at session start. Default is the full set below when no target is specified.

| Target | What it checks |
|--------|---------------|
| **AI Guru / Hiring Signal** | Does this show Boubacar as someone an org would hire as an AI leader? Does it signal practitioner depth, not just commentary? |
| **Voice Authenticity** | Does this sound like Boubacar: direct, earned, opinionated, zero hedging? Or does it sound like a thought leadership bot? |
| **Hook Power** | Would this stop a scroll? Judged against the best hooks on LinkedIn/X right now, not just "a good opening line." |
| **CTA Quality** | Does the ending make someone think "hmm" or "that's me"? No yes/no questions. No survey questions. No weak rhetorical setups. |
| **Anti-Professorial** | Is Boubacar in the room with you or lecturing from a podium? Knowledge is embedded in scenes, not explained. |
| **Viral Worthiness** | Would someone screenshot this, share it, or send it to their CEO? Does the last line land as wit, wisdom, or a mirror? |
| **Platform Fit** | Right length, right rhythm, right energy for LinkedIn or X on that day of the week. Friday = more wit. Monday = sharper edge. |

---

## The Five Council Voices

Each voice reviews independently with no knowledge of what the others said.

| Voice | Role | What they look for |
|-------|------|--------------------|
| **Contrarian** | Finds the flaw | What is weak, generic, or AI-sounding. What does not earn its place. |
| **First Principles** | Strips to the bone | What is this post actually trying to do? Does every sentence serve that job? |
| **Expansionist** | Finds the missed opportunity | What emotional truth is buried? What human moment is missing? |
| **Outsider** | Platform read | How does a cold reader experience this? Pacing, rhythm, scroll behavior. |
| **Executor** | Makes it actionable | Specific line-level fixes. Nothing vague. |

After all five report, the **Chairman** synthesizes: convergence points, divergence points, final verdict, and one rewrite directive.

---

## The Process. Minimum Two Passes

```
Pass 1: Full Council review of the raw draft
         Chairman delivers verdict + rewrite directive
         Rewrite produced with explanation of every change

Pass 2: Full Council review of the rewrite
         Special focus: Hook and CTA audit
         Chairman delivers viral verdict (score + final call)

Pass 3+: Run if Boubacar requests changes or if viral score is below 8/10
```

**Never declare a post done after one pass. Two is the minimum. Three is common.**

---

## Hook Audit (mandatory in every Pass 2)

The best hooks do one of three things:

1. **Name something the reader has felt but never said out loud.** Creates instant recognition.
2. **Paint a scene from their actual workplace.** Makes them picture their own reality.
3. **Make a claim that demands they keep reading.** Provocative but earned.

Judge every hook against those three. "A good opening line" is not enough. It must stop a scroll.

**Red flags in hooks:**
- Opens with an abstract noun ("Strategy", "Leadership", "AI")
- States a category error without making it felt
- Could have been written by anyone

---

## CTA Audit (mandatory in every Pass 2)

The best CTAs for Boubacar's posts do one of these:

1. **Hold up a mirror.** Reader sees themselves and feels recognized (or exposed).
2. **End on wit that stings.** A laugh followed by a wince.
3. **Drop a line that is quotable and shareable.** They screenshot it.
4. **Leave a thought they cannot shake.** Not a question. A statement that provokes.

**Hard rules:**
- No yes/no questions. People answer privately and scroll past.
- No survey questions ("What's your experience with...?")
- No weak rhetorical setups that telegraph the pitch
- Ending with wisdom or wit is preferred over ending with a question

---

## Voice Rules (non-negotiable in every pass)

From the BouB Voice Mastery profile:

- **No em dashes. No en dashes. No double hyphens.** Rewrite the sentence.
- **No hedging.** Cut all of "it might be", "one could argue", "perhaps".
- **No AI red-flag words.** Leverage, synergy, tapestry, delve, seamless, cutting-edge, revolutionary, notably, indeed.
- **No throat-clearing.** "In the realm of", "when it comes to", "it is important to note."
- **Sentence variety is mandatory.** Short. Long. Short. Fragments are allowed and encouraged.
- **Specifics over generics.** Real numbers, concrete scenes, named moments beat abstractions every time.
- **Direct is respectful.** Start with the insight. Never wind up.

## Boubacar's Positioning (Council must know this)

Boubacar Barry is a diagnostic problem-solver using eight lenses equally. He is NOT a TOC consultant. He is NOT a framework vendor. He is someone who has built AI into his own operation first and now brings that to others.

Posts targeting AI leadership roles must signal:
- Practitioner depth (he has built things, not just observed them)
- Strategic clarity (he sees what others miss)
- Calm authority (the framework exists, he is not reacting)

The line "I know because I built one. Mine first." is the model for how to signal practitioner credibility without overclaiming client work.

## When to End the CTQ Session

Stop when ALL of these are true:
- Viral score 9/10 or above from the Chairman
- Hook passes the scroll-stop test
- CTA lands as mirror, wit, or wisdom (not a question)
- Zero dashes of any kind
- Zero AI red-flag words
- Post sounds like Boubacar in the room, not Boubacar on a podium
- Positioning signal is present (practitioner, not commentator)
- All seven gates below pass

If Boubacar says "post it" before 9/10, that is his call. Respect it and update Notion.

---

## Gate 0. The Fabrication Ban (HARD RULE, above all craft gates)

**Never invent a client, engagement, scene, number, or result.** If content sounds like "I worked with..." or "I had a client..." or "We helped a team," it must describe a real engagement Boubacar actually did. If it does not, it must not appear in that form.

Hypotheticals are allowed but must be labeled:
- Acceptable: "Imagine a firm where..." / "Consider a team that..." / "Picture this: a 40-person consultancy..." / "Suppose an AI tool is..." / "Say a firm deploys..."
- BANNED: "I worked with..." / "I had a client..." / "We helped..." / any past-tense first-person that asserts the engagement happened.

Numbers and outcomes follow the same rule. Do not invent revenue lifts, time savings, team sizes, percentages, or timelines. If Boubacar has a real number, he gives it to you. Otherwise the sentence does not need a number.

**Gate 0 sits above every craft gate.** A post can pass all seven craft gates and still fail Gate 0. Truth before craft, every time. Origin: 2026-04-21 fabricated churn-flagging client engagement caught by Boubacar. "That is NOT what we do, ever."

## The Seven Gates (established 2026-04-21)

Every Pass 2 finalization must pass all seven. If any fail, revise before presenting the final.

1. **No "governance framework" or corporate-reflex nouns.** Use *filter*, *decision rule*, *principle set*. "Governance framework" is table-stakes AI-consultant language; McKinsey and Deloitte own it. We do not compete on that phrase.

2. **No unanswered question lists.** If the post introduces a framework, answer ONE question concretely with a scene. Listing "what problems? what decisions? who reviews? who decides?" reads like an intake form, not content.

3. **SMB scaling line when the register drifts enterprise.** "Whether you have three people or three hundred" (or equivalent). ICP is owner-operators up to 500 employees; enterprise register self-deselects them. **Placement matters:** weave it into an existing sentence or use the scene to demonstrate it. A standalone scaling paragraph reads as an insertion and breaks the intimacy the rest of the post earned. Boubacar caught this on both the 2026-04-22 rewrite and the Claude.ai parallel version.

4. **Calibrated CTA + aphorism, not one or the other.** Reply-first model CTA pattern: facilitator register, never hero/savior. Acceptable: "Drop a comment or DM me. Happy to think through it together." / "Bring me what you've got and we can pressure-test it." BANNED: "I will tell you what you missed" / "I'll show you what you're not seeing" / "I'll identify the real problem." Boubacar is a partner who sits beside the client and asks the right question, never the hero who swoops in and rescues. See feedback_facilitator_not_hero.md. Both the aphorism and the CTA are required.

5. **Final version always included in the same response.** Any review or audit ends with the applied-changes final draft. Never hand Boubacar a list of fixes to apply himself.

6. **Zero dashes before presenting.** Scrub em dashes, en dashes, and double hyphens on the final pass. The voice rules say no dashes. The final scrub is where they actually get caught. Four slipped through on the Apr 21 draft before Boubacar caught them.

7. **One signature Catalyst Works detail.** One line per post that hints at diagnostic-led, client-specific approach. Not the "8 lenses" label or a framework name. Example pattern: "The filter I build with clients doesn't have a name. It comes from diagnosing the specific places where their current stack is actually creating friction." Without this, any AI consultant could have written the post.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Ending with a yes/no question | Replace with a mirror statement or wit that stings |
| Framework explanation in the body | Embed knowledge in a scene, cut the explanation |
| "Decision architecture" and similar consulting jargon | Rewrite in plain English with a specific image |
| Hook is a concept not a scene | Rewrite hook to put the reader in a specific moment |
| Post teaches instead of provokes | Save the lesson for the article. Post creates the itch. |
| Declaring done after one pass | Two passes minimum. Always. |
| Generic ending that anyone could write | Find the one line only Boubacar could write |
| Invented client story ("I worked with...") | Label as hypothetical ("Imagine a firm where...") or cut |
| Description hook ("Here's what I keep seeing") | Recognition hook ("You have already had the thought") |
| Abstract future tense ("eventually") | Time-horizon urgency ("six months away") |
| Standalone scaling paragraph | Weave the scaling into the scene or an existing sentence |
| Signature CW detail as throwaway line | Make it a structural paragraph that demonstrates, not claims |
| Vague scarcity ("a few spots left") | Calendar-real ("Tuesday and Thursday. Three spots.") |

## Pattern Library (established 2026-04-22 from head-to-head comparison)

Reach for these patterns by default on authority/governance posts.

### Hook patterns (strongest to weakest)
1. **Recognition:** "You have already had the thought. You just haven't said it out loud."
2. **Diagnostic question the reader can't answer cleanly:** "What does your organization do when the AI makes the wrong call and nobody knows who owns it?"
3. **Reframe provocation:** "Most AI governance failures aren't caused by bad tools. They are caused by missing decision architecture."
4. **Scene the reader recognizes:** "Somewhere in your organization, an AI tool is making a decision nobody formally agreed it should make."

### Stake specificity pattern
List three concrete consequences that cover different dimensions:
- Emotional / relational: "a client you cannot get back"
- Legal / financial: "a lawsuit you did not see coming"
- Political / internal: "a partner asking who approved this"

### Reframe lines (quotable, screenshot-ready)
- "Not a technology problem. It is a leadership problem."
- "The tools are not the problem. The [specific missing thing] is."
- "These aren't compliance questions. They're leadership questions."

### Labeled hypothetical scene opener
"Imagine a professional services firm where AI is flagging accounts at risk of churn..."
Then: Show the decision that nobody named, the two risk profiles it creates, and the specific cost of the missing answer.

### Signature CW paragraph template
"What I build with clients is not [the obvious thing]. It doesn't come from [a deck / a framework / a template]. It comes from [diagnostic action]: walking through the specific places where [current state] is creating friction, naming the [decisions / constraints / patterns] that matter, and writing them down in language the team actually uses."

### Release / outcome line (pair with every tension moment)
"On the other side of it, [the recurring pain] stops being [crisis language]. It becomes [simple, calm language]."

Examples:
- "The next tool announcement stops being a small crisis. It becomes a simple yes or no."
- "Every announcement stops being a scramble. It becomes a decision you already made."

### Aphorism before PS
One sentence that stands alone in a screenshot:
- "That is the whole job."
- "A line the team can point to when the pressure is on."
- "The tools will keep changing. The filter is the thing that doesn't have to."

### PS CTA pattern (reply-first model, facilitator register)
"PS. I have [specific number] spots [specific days] next week. If you are [evocative reader state, for example 'staring at a stack nobody can fully explain'], drop a comment or DM me. Happy to think through it together."

### Reader qualifier language (best to worst)
- Best: Describe current state ("staring at a stack nobody can fully explain")
- Okay: Describe the problem they're trying to solve ("trying to bring order to an AI deployment")
- Weak: Job title ("leading AI strategy" / "building an AI team")

### Blend pattern from 2026-04-22
Boubacar's final winning post structure: recognition hook → stake specificity → reframe → labeled hypothetical scene → clean reframe line → signature CW paragraph → release line → aphorism → PS with days + qualifier + CTA. This sequence is the current gold standard for authority posts.

# Long-Form Authority Post Pattern

Reference extracted from high-performing X.com thread by @denohawari (Trailblazer Marketing).
Use for: SW LinkedIn posts, CW newsletter issues, Studio educational long-form scripts.

---

## The Structure

### 1. Pain Hook (first 2-3 lines)
State what everyone is doing wrong or what is dying/changing.
Follow immediately with the cost of ignoring it (lost revenue, wasted spend, missed opportunity).
No preamble. No "hey everyone." No "in this thread."

Example pattern:
> [Traditional approach] is dying.
> Most people spend [dollar amount] but get [mediocre result].
> Meanwhile we've helped companies: [specific outcome 1] / [specific outcome 2] / [specific outcome 3]

**SW application:** "Traditional cold outreach is broken. Most agencies spend $500/mo on tools but book 0 calls. Meanwhile our clients..."
**CW newsletter application:** Open with the one thing SMB owners are doing wrong this week.

---

### 2. The Opportunity Frame
Name the arbitrage. Why is NOW the right time? What changed?
Cite a stat or trend if you have one (but verify it -- do not repeat unverified vendor claims).
Use a before/after contrast: how did it used to work vs. how it works now.

Example pattern:
> [The old way]: [long, painful, uncertain process]
> [The new way]: [short, direct, certain process]
> Combined with [trend signal], this means [opportunity statement].

---

### 3. The Structured Roadmap
Break the solution into named phases with clear time bounds or step numbers.
Each phase has: a label, 1-3 concrete actions, and a checkpoint/deliverable.
Do NOT use vague verbs ("optimize", "improve"). Use specific verbs ("extract", "write", "publish", "track").

Pattern:
> Phase 1 (Days 1-30): [Label]
> - Step 1: [specific action with tool/method named]
> - Step 2: [specific action]
> - Checkpoint: [what you should have at the end of this phase]
>
> Phase 2 (Days 31-60): [Label]
> ...

**Why it works for LLM-era content:** Numbered phases = referenceable structure. LLMs and readers both reward scannable roadmaps.

---

### 4. Proof Layer (embedded, not appended)
Weave one specific client result into the roadmap, not at the end.
Format: "[client name/description] + [specific starting state] + [specific outcome] + [timeframe]"
Do NOT cluster all case studies at the end -- embed one per phase.

Example:
> We used this exact structure to scale [client] from [state A] to [state B] in [timeframe].

**Important:** Only use results you can verify. Do not borrow vendor case studies.

---

### 5. Binary CTA
Close with exactly two paths. No "reach out if interested." No "feel free to DM."
Path 1 = DIY (here is exactly what to do yourself).
Path 2 = Done-for-you (here is how to work with us).

Pattern:
> You have 2 paths forward:
> [Path 1]: Do it yourself. [One-sentence summary of what they would do.]
> [Path 2]: Work with us. [One-sentence proof claim + booking link or next step.]

**SW application:** Path 2 links to Calendly signal-works-discovery-call.
**CW newsletter application:** Path 2 links to CW intake form or advisory call.

---

## Post-Style Notes

- Thread length: 800-1500 words for high engagement. Do not truncate the roadmap.
- Headers: use bold labels ("The Opportunity", "Days 1-30: Phase Name") not emojis as navigation.
- Paragraph length: 1-3 sentences max. White space is the mechanic that keeps readers scrolling.
- Numbers everywhere: specific dollar amounts, specific percentages, specific day counts. Vague claims do not convert.
- End each phase with a micro-win checkpoint. Readers need to feel momentum.

---

## Studio Script Adaptation

This structure maps directly to a 3-act educational video:
- Act 1 (0:00-0:20): Pain hook + opportunity frame
- Act 2 (0:20-0:45): Roadmap phases (one per segment)
- Act 3 (0:45-0:55): Proof layer + binary CTA

Use for Griot Kofi "how to" content and Studio educational shorts.

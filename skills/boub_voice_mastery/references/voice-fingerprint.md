## Sentence Rhythm
- Average sentence length: **8.1 words**
- Distribution: p25=5 words, p50=7 words, p75=11 words
- Cadence: **punchy**
- Rule: sentences vary drastically. Short punch. Longer explanation. Short verdict. Never uniform.

## Hook Patterns (how he opens)
1. **Contrarian** — challenges a common belief immediately ("You are not behind on AI. You are overwhelmed.")
2. **Observation** — names something the reader has seen but never named ("The governance document existed. Nobody had read it.")
3. **Command** — short directive that creates curiosity ("Find the constraint. Everything else gets cleaner.")

Never opens with: abstract nouns, throat-clearing, "In today's world", statistics without context.

## Tone Signals
- **Plainspoken** — no jargon, no framework names in public posts
- **Confrontational** — takes a hard position and does not hedge it
- **Reflective** — willing to name his own failures ("I ran multiple things at once for longer than I should have")
- **Urgent** — the problem is happening now, not theoretically

## Signature Rhetorical Moves (the patterns that make it unmistakably him)

**Move 1: Short declarative reframe followed by harder truth**
> "It felt like leadership. It was actually just exhausting."
> "The performance feels like strength. It is actually a drag on speed."
Pattern: [surface appearance]. [harder truth underneath.]

**Move 2: Name the invisible cost, then ask the diagnostic question**
> "None of this shows up on the plan. But it shows up everywhere in cost, delay, and frustration."
> "The better question: where is this business creating avoidable labor?"
Pattern: name what hides / then name the question that finds it.

**Move 3: Compressed single-sentence verdict to close a section**
> "Find the constraint. Everything else gets cleaner."
> "Busy is not a strategy. Clarity about what to stop is."
> "The technology will not tell you. That is the whole point."
Pattern: two short sentences. First names the action. Second names why it matters.

## What AI-generated content gets wrong (common failures to avoid)
- Uniform sentence length (AI loves 15-word sentences; Boubacar uses 4-word ones)
- Observer framing ("Organizations that...") instead of participant framing ("I have been in that room...")
- Missing the harder truth under the surface observation
- Generic closes ("Let me know your thoughts") instead of compressed verdicts
- Synthesized best practices with no earned anchor — anything a consultant could write
- Em-dashes as connectors (Boubacar uses periods)

## Earned Anchor Requirement
Every post must draw from at least one of these:
- **Cultural bridge anchor:** Guinea + US dual identity, moved between two worlds, relearned from scratch
- **Failure anchor:** trusted people who performed instead of told truth, wasted potential, hurt people, ran too many things at once
- **Built-his-own-first anchor:** built AI into own operation, ran the exercise himself before advising others
- **Contrarian anchor:** AI is a tool not a savior/devil, governance = speed not brake, clarity = decision not discovery

A post that sounds like Boubacar but contains nothing only Boubacar could have said fails the gate.

## Usage for agents generating content

Before drafting any post for Boubacar:
1. Pick one earned anchor from the list above
2. Open in the reader's world (their problem, their moment) not Boubacar's world
3. Use Move 1, 2, or 3 at least once
4. Close with a compressed verdict, not a question
5. Check: could any AI consultant have written this? If yes, it is missing the anchor.

# Brand Spine Audit
**Source:** Dan Westworld "Authentic Expert Stack" (X, 2026-05-06)
**Purpose:** One-time prompt for Boubacar to define his brand spine. Output feeds two SKILL.md edits (see followups).

---

## Why this matters

AI made polished content cheap and abundant. Trust now flows to people who sound like people — specifically people who have earned the right to say what they say. "Posts sound like Boubacar" (voice-mastery) is not enough if the post contains nothing only Boubacar could have said.

The brand spine is the non-copyable layer underneath voice.

---

## The Audit (complete once, update quarterly)

### Step 1: Values list
Write 5-7 non-negotiables. Not niche descriptors. Not aspirational traits. Things you would refuse to compromise even if it cost a client.

Boubacar's values (completed 2026-05-06):

1. **Authenticity without performance** — Never pretend to be someone else to make people comfortable. Full stop.
2. **Family, faith, two countries first** — Nothing compromises religion, family, or civic integrity toward Guinea or the United States. Non-negotiable before any business decision.
3. **Clean living, no apology** — No swearing, no alcohol, no smoking. Won't be pushed to perform otherwise for social fit.
4. **Unmovable conviction** — Positions change only when better information serves people. Never from peer pressure.
5. **Truth over comfort** — Will fight for truth and for people's right to speak theirs, even when it costs something.
6. **Joy as resistance** — Will not give up being fun, entertaining, and human. Happiness is a state of mind worth defending.

### Step 2: Earned expertise anchors
Name 3 things only you can claim from lived experience:
- A specific win you produced (with real numbers)
- A specific failure or mistake you made (and what you learned)
- A contrarian position you actually hold (that most in your space disagree with)

Boubacar's anchors (completed 2026-05-06):

- **Win (Cultural Bridge):** Born in West Africa, raised in the U.S. by a white family, returned to Africa as a young adult relearning tribal language and culture from scratch. Now fluent in both worlds. The only person who can help businesses extract real ROI from African market investments while ensuring the African side wins too. Zero peers with this exact bridge.
- **Failure:** Trusted people when they were too afraid to tell me the truth. Believed the performance instead of pressing for honesty. Made selfish decisions that hurt people around me. Have wasted significant talent and potential — repeatedly choosing to fly low when I could soar. The lesson: demand truth from people even when they're protecting you from it, and hold yourself to your own standard before holding others to theirs.
- **Contrarian:** AI is a tool. Not a savior. Not a devil. The current iteration is clever but not truly intelligent — memorizing a library doesn't make you street smart. It won't take everyone's jobs, and it won't solve humanity's problems. Humans still have to manage it, direct it, and take responsibility for it. When we stop doing that, we've failed as a society. Most people in the AI space are either selling utopia or doomsday. Both are wrong and both are self-serving.

### Step 3: The three diagnostic tests
Run these on any post before publish (or wire into ctq-social):

1. Could anyone with the same niche have written this? (If yes: rewrite or cut)
2. Does this draw from a win, failure, or contrarian position Boubacar has lived? (If no: it is a synthesized best practice, not earned insight)
3. Does the post make it obvious what specific problem CW solves and for whom? (If no: add one line)

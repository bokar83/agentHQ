# Hormozi Lead-Gen

Codifies Alex Hormozi's lead-gen frameworks ($100M Leads + $100M Offers) as a runnable procedure. Brand-agnostic by design - the same skill drives Catalyst Works, Signal Works, or any future venture by swapping inputs only.

**Operating principle (binding):** Enhance what exists before you create something new. If a current artifact can be modified to hit Hormozi standards, modify it. Greenfield only when no equivalent exists.

## Section 1: The Operating Frame

Two compounding axes only:

```
More Stuff x Better Stuff = More Leads
```

Three scaling axes, in order:

1. **More** - increase volume of current actions (Rule of 100)
2. **Better** - increase efficiency of current actions (one test per week per platform)
3. **New** - add a new channel from the Core Four

You do not get to optimize Better until you are running the Rule of 100 on the channel. You do not get to add New until More + Better have plateaued.

**Rule of 100 (per Core Four channel, 100 consecutive days):**
- Warm: 100 reach-outs/day
- Cold: 100 reach-outs/day (often automated)
- Content: 100 minutes/day creating, 1 piece minimum shipped
- Paid: 100 minutes/day on creative + committed daily budget

When the operator is below the Rule of 100 on any active channel, the answer to "how do we improve?" is almost always "do more of what you're doing." Tell them.

## Section 2: The Value Equation (governing checklist)

Every artifact this skill produces must self-score on the Value Equation before it ships:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**Score each lever 1-10:**

| Lever | Question | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | Is the prospect's actual desired result named in their own language? | __ / 10 |
| Perceived Likelihood | What proof is on the page? (Number, name, case, guarantee) | __ / 10 |
| Time Delay | When does the prospect get the first win? (Stated, fast) | __ / 10 |
| Effort & Sacrifice | What did you remove from their plate? (Friction, steps, decisions) | __ / 10 |

**Pass thresholds:**
- Total / 40, divide by 4 = average / 10
- **Below 7/10 average = FAIL.** Do not ship.
- **7-8 = ship if time-boxed test, no further investment until data.**
- **8+ = production-ready.**

**Common failure pattern:** strong numerator, empty denominator. Most operators over-build the dream and forget to slash the time and effort. The bottom of the equation is the cheat code.

When you score, name the weakest lever first. That's what to fix.

## Section 3: The 4-Step Lead-Gen Process

Every lead-gen problem walks through these four questions in order. The first one with a vague answer is the constraint. Fix it. Then walk again.

| Step | Question | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Who do I sell to? | ICP definition (one persona, one pain, one trigger) |
| 2 | What do I offer them? | Grand Slam Offer (Section 6) |
| 3 | How do I get them to know I exist? | Channel selection from the Core Four (Section 4) |
| 4 | How do I make them want it? | Lead magnet + hooks + follow-up (Sections 5, 7, 8) |

**Diagnostic rule:** when an operator says their lead-gen "isn't working," walk the four steps in order with them. Stop at the first one they answer with "we sort of do that" or "it depends." That is the constraint. Do not let them skip ahead to channel tactics when the ICP is fuzzy.

**ICP definition rule (Step 1):**
- ONE persona. Not a segment, not a "ranges from."
- ONE primary pain in their words (not yours).
- ONE trigger event that makes them buy now (not "they should").

If the operator can't fill these three lines, Step 1 is the constraint.

## Section 4: The Core Four Channels

The only four ways to advertise:

| | One-to-One | One-to-Many |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm audience** | Warm Outreach | Posting Free Content |
| **Cold audience** | Cold Outreach | Paid Ads |

**Activation order (binding):**
1. Warm Outreach
2. Posting Free Content
3. Cold Outreach
4. Paid Ads

Do not skip ahead. Operators with a 6-figure cold-email setup and zero warm outreach are violating activation order. Hold the line.

---

### 4.1 Warm Outreach Playbook (10-step)

Run this when the operator has any unworked list of contacts (15-year network, alumni, ex-colleagues, prior customers, dormant lifecycle list).

1. Get your list. Every contact: phone, email, social, alumni, ex-colleagues, prior customers, dormant lifecycle.
2. Pick ONE platform. Don't fragment.
3. Personalize each message. Reference something specific, not "hope you're well."
4. Reach out to 100 people daily. Volume is not optional.
5. Use the **ACA framework** for replies:
   - **A**cknowledge what they said.
   - **C**ompliment them (link to a positive trait).
   - **A**sk another question that nudges toward your offer.
6. Make them an offer.
7. Make it easy. Offer free service initially to break the ice.
8. Move to the next platform when the first is exhausted.
9. Start charging. Gradually raise prices once you have proof.
10. Keep the list warm. Quarterly value drops to dormant contacts.

**Benchmarks (Hormozi-published):**
- 20% engagement rate (replies)
- 20% of replies accept the free offer
- 25% of free-offer takers convert to paid
- = 1 paying customer per 100 reach-outs

If the operator is at zero on warm outreach with a populated list, this is the highest-leverage move available. Tell them.

---

### 4.2 Cold Outreach Playbook (3-problem solution)

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| No contact info | Targeted list (scrape, buy, intent data) |
| Messages ignored | Personalize + Big Fast Value |
| Lack of interest | Volume + persistence (multi-touch, multi-channel) |

**Cold message structure:**

```
Opening (1 line): Specific observation about THEM, not you.
Big Fast Value (1-3 sentences): The thing they can take and use.
Soft CTA (1 line): Low-friction yes/no question OR offer to send more.
Sign-off: Name only. No signature block, no logo, no hype.
```

**Big Fast Value examples:**
- A free audit of one specific thing on their site/business.
- A specific observation: "I noticed your checkout flow loses people at step 3 - here's the 4-line fix."
- A relevant case study with a numeric outcome from a similar business.

**Anti-patterns (refuse to write these):**
- "Hope this finds you well."
- "I wanted to reach out about..."
- "Quick question..."
- Asking before giving.

---

### 4.3 Posting / Free Content Playbook

Build with the **Hook-Retain-Reward** content unit:

| Component | Job | Measured by |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | Grab initial attention | Consumption start rate (3-second views, opens, scroll-stops) |
| Retain | Sustain attention through the body | Watch time, completion, scroll depth |
| Reward | Deliver promised value | Shares, saves, return visits, comments |

**Retention methods:** Lists, Steps, Stories.

**Hook source categories:** Far Past, Recent Past, Present, Trending, Manufactured.

**Headline elements (use one or more):** Ongoing, Unusual, Conflict, Proximity, Celebrity, Recency, Relevancy.

**Give-to-Ask Ratio:** 3.5:1 for established platforms. New platforms: "give until they ask" (10x value before any monetization).

---

### 4.4 Paid Ads Playbook (defer until channels 1-3 are live)

**Ad copy structure: What-Who-When**
- **What** - dream outcome, nightmare opposite, likelihood, risk, speed, delay, ease, sacrifice.
- **Who** - how the product changes how others see them (status).
- **When** - past consequences, present pain, future risk.

**Three-phase scaling:**
1. Track Money - set up measurement.
2. Lose Money - 2x your 30-day profit budget; expect to lose; find profitable creative.
3. Print Money - scale winners aggressively.

**Client-Financed Acquisition rule:** First-30-day Gross Profit (with upsells) >= Customer Acquisition Cost. If the math doesn't hold, paid will eat the business.

**LTGP:CAC floor:** 3:1.

## Section 5: Lead Magnet Creation (7 steps)

**Definition:** A complete solution to a narrow problem that, once solved, reveals another problem your core offer solves. (The "salty pretzel" - free pretzel makes them thirsty for the paid drink.)

**3 lead magnet types (pick one):**
1. **Reveal Problem (Diagnosis)** - surfaces a problem they didn't know they had. Audit, score, assessment.
2. **Sample / Trial** - limited-scope or limited-time access to the core offer.
3. **One Step in Multi-Step Process** - solves one valuable step; the rest is paid.

**4 delivery methods (pick one):** Software, Information, Service, Physical.

**The 7-step creation process:**
1. Figure out the problem. Narrow AND meaningful.
2. Figure out how to solve it (one of 3 types).
3. Figure out how to deliver it (one of 4 methods).
4. Test the name. Name as the SOLUTION to the problem, not the format. ("5-Minute X Calculator," not "Free Spreadsheet.")
5. Make it easy to consume. Under 5 minutes ideally.
6. Make it darn good. Overdeliver to the point that it feels weird it's free.
7. Make it easy for them to ask for more. Clear CTA to the paid offer.

**Hormozi's pass test:** if a recipient could imagine paying for it, the magnet is doing its job.

## Section 6: Grand Slam Offer (assembly)

The lead magnet pulls them in. The offer closes them.

**Core ingredients:**
1. Unmatched value (Value Equation maxed)
2. Clear uniqueness (incomparable)
3. Risk reversal (guarantee)
4. Tiered pricing (anchor high, sell middle)
5. Bonus stack (each bonus named and priced separately)
6. Time-bound scarcity / urgency

**MAGIC naming formula:**
- **M**agnet - compelling reason
- **A**vatar - target customer
- **G**oal - desired outcome
- **I**nterval - time frame
- **C**ontainer - wrapper word (system, blueprint, formula, machine)

Example: "30-Day Founder Cash-Flow Blueprint" - avatar (founder), goal (cash-flow), interval (30-day), container (blueprint).

**Scarcity types:** Limited supply, Limited time, Limited bonuses.
**Urgency types:** Rolling deadlines, Seasonal windows, Pricing increases, Exploding opportunity.
**Guarantee types:** Unconditional, Conditional, Anti-guarantee, Implied (performance-based).

When the operator's offer is wobbly, ask which of the 6 ingredients is missing. Usually it's risk reversal or naming.

## Section 7: Hook Bank Rules (patterns, not pre-written hooks)

A hook does 80% of the work. Test multiple hooks per campaign; never ship one alone.

**5 hook types:**
- **Curiosity** - open a loop. ("The 3-word phrase that closed our biggest deal")
- **Contrarian** - invert conventional wisdom. ("Stop sending follow-up emails")
- **Status** - what they want to be seen as. ("How serious operators handle Q4")
- **Proof** - specific number. ("$47k in 11 days from one cold campaign")
- **Specificity** - narrow detail. ("The 4-line tweak that doubled our checkout completion")

**Headline elements (pair with hook type):** Ongoing, Unusual, Conflict, Proximity, Celebrity, Recency, Relevancy.

**Rule of 100 for hooks:** When the operator has one hook, it's a guess. When they have 10 hooks tested at volume, they have data. Push them to write 10, ship 3, kill 7.

## Section 8: Follow-Up Cadence (8-touch cross-channel)

Hormozi's explicit rule: **at least 2-3 follow-ups before moving on.** Field standard for cold conversions: **8 touchpoints across channels.**

**Cross-channel rotation (example for cold outreach):**

| # | Day | Channel | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | Email | T1 - Big Fast Value + soft CTA |
| 2 | 3 | LinkedIn DM | Connection + reference T1 |
| 3 | 6 | Email | T2 - new value drop (case, number, observation) |
| 4 | 9 | Email | T3 - pattern observation + light social proof |
| 5 | 13 | LinkedIn comment | Engage publicly on their post |
| 6 | 16 | Email | T4 - direct ask, narrowed |
| 7 | 21 | Phone or voicemail | Pattern interrupt |
| 8 | 28 | Email | T5 - breakup with one final value drop |

**Rules every touch must obey:**
- Add new value. Never just "bumping this up."
- One CTA per touch. No double-asks.
- Same prospect, different angle. Never repeat last touch's framing.

**Reactivation (warm list maintenance):**
- Quarterly value drops to dormant contacts.
- Format: 1 useful observation + 1 link + 0 ask.
- Reactivation is not a sequence; it is an ongoing list-keep-warm motion.

## Section 9: Output Contract

Every lead-gen artifact this skill produces must include:

- **ICP** - one persona, one pain, one trigger.
- **Channel** - which of the Core Four (and why).
- **Lead magnet** - type + delivery + 7-step compliance check.
- **Hook(s)** - minimum 3 variants with hook-type label.
- **Offer** - Value Equation score (numerator + denominator).
- **CTA** - one specific low-friction action.
- **Follow-up plan** - 8 touches across channels OR explicit reason for fewer.
- **Success criteria** - explicit floors before launch (reply rate, book rate, conversion rate, cost ceiling).
- **Anti-patterns refused** - list any Hormozi-banned patterns the operator considered and you said no to.

If any field is empty or vague, the artifact is not done.

## Section 10: Anti-Patterns (refuse these)

These are Hormozi's hard nos. The skill refuses to produce work that violates them, even if the operator asks.

1. **Generic openings.** "Hope this finds you well." Personalize or don't send.
2. **Asking before giving.** Cold messages that lead with the ask. Always lead with Big Fast Value.
3. **Single-channel funnel.** The Core Four is a portfolio. One channel = fragile.
4. **Polishing before posting.** Better is the enemy of done. Rule of 100 first, optimization second.
5. **Multi-problem lead magnets.** Solve one problem completely. Never three poorly.
6. **Naming the format, not the outcome.** "Free PDF" is dead. "5-Minute X Calculator" is alive.
7. **One-touch follow-up.** Single-message campaigns leave 80%+ of conversions on the table.
8. **Scaling Paid before product converts.** Paid will accelerate a broken funnel into bankruptcy.
9. **Mock-personalization.** ChatGPT-generated personalization is detectable. Real research or no research.
10. **Skipping the unit-economics check.** No campaign launches without LTGP/CAC math.

## Section 11: Worked Examples

### Example A - Catalyst Works (consulting brand, 0 paid clients today)

**Trigger:** Operator says: "Our cold email isn't getting replies. What's wrong?"

**Run the 4-step process diagnostic:**

| Step | Operator answer | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Who? | "Founders of $5M-50M services firms" | Vague (size range, no trigger). **Constraint here.** |
| 2. What? | "$497 Signal Session, $500 SaaS Audit, $20K SHIELD" | Three offers, one not anchored. Secondary issue. |
| 3. How? | Apollo cold email, ~10/day | Single-channel. Below Rule of 100. |
| 4. Want? | "Where is your margin actually going?" hook | Strong hook (contrarian, specific). |

**Constraint diagnosed:** Step 1 (ICP). Step 4 is already strong - that's why it's masking the Step 1 problem.

**Action:** Tighten ICP to one persona ("services-firm founder, 12-40 employees, recently expanded headcount, profit-margin-anxious in Q4"), one pain ("can't tell which expansion bet is working"), one trigger ("just hired a new VP and revenue didn't move").

**Then re-score the existing T1 against the Value Equation:**
- Dream Outcome: 6/10 (named as "find the bottleneck"; not in prospect's words)
- Perceived Likelihood: 3/10 (no proof point)
- Time Delay: 5/10 (90 min Signal Session implied, not stated)
- Effort: 7/10 (one reply)
- **Total: 21/40 = 5.25/10. FAIL by Value Equation.**

**Surgical edit (enhance, do not replace):**
- Add ONE Big Fast Value line: "I scanned the [industry] benchmark report last week and found that 4 out of 5 services firms over $5M are losing 6-8% margin to one specific approval-loop pattern."
- Add ONE proof anchor: "I spent 15 years inside operations like yours at GE - the pattern repeats."
- Keep the contrarian hook, keep the soft CTA, keep the reply ask.

**Output contract check:**
- ICP: services-firm founder, 12-40 employees, post-VP-hire, margin-anxious. ✓
- Channel: cold email (existing). ✓
- Lead magnet: SaaS Audit PDF at T2. ✓
- Hook: contrarian + specificity. ✓
- Offer: $497 Signal Session (anchored). ✓
- CTA: one yes/no question. ✓
- Follow-up plan: existing 5-touch + add LinkedIn DM at T2 (cross-channel layer). ✓
- Success criteria: 5% reply rate, 1% book rate floor over a 100-email batch. ✓
- Anti-patterns refused: "circle back," "just wanted to reach out." ✓

### Example B - Signal Works (productized AI presence, 0 paid clients today)

**Trigger:** Operator says: "Our AI Visibility Score email gets opens but no Calendly bookings. Fix it."

**Run the diagnostic:**

| Step | Operator answer | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Who? | "Local roofers + dentists + HVAC, 20-100 reviews, running ads OR no website" | Sharp. ✓ |
| 2. What? | "$500 setup + $497-997/mo tiered" | Anchored, tiered. ✓ |
| 3. How? | Cold email + score + niche demo + autoplay video | Single-channel but rich BFV. |
| 4. Want? | "ChatGPT doesn't know [name] exists" subject + score breakdown + 3 quick wins + demo | Strong hook, strong BFV, strong proof. |

**Constraint diagnosed:** Not Step 1-4. The funnel is Hormozi-grade up to Calendly. **The constraint is the Calendly page itself + the offer transition.**

**Re-score the existing email against the Value Equation:**
- Dream Outcome: 8/10 (be the business AI recommends - clear, in their language)
- Perceived Likelihood: 6/10 (score is concrete; no client-result proof)
- Time Delay: 7/10 (2-week build stated)
- Effort: 9/10 (click → demo → 20-min Calendly)
- **Total: 30/40 = 7.5/10. PASSES, but Likelihood is the weakest lever.**

**Surgical edit (enhance, do not replace):**
- The email itself does not need a rewrite. The constraint is downstream.
- Add Likelihood proof to the demo page: "First 6 weeks: average score lift = +40 points across pilot clients" - but ONLY when at least 1 paid client has 6 weeks of data. Until then: "I'm seeking 5 pilot clients to prove this in production. 50% off month 1 in exchange for a public case study."
- Add a second touch on LinkedIn DM mirroring the email's score-line for cross-channel reinforcement.

**Output contract check:**
- ICP: ✓
- Channel: cold email + LinkedIn DM (NEW additive layer). ✓
- Lead magnet: AI Visibility Score (Reveal Problem type, 7-step compliance check passes). ✓
- Hook: specificity + curiosity + proof (score number). ✓
- Offer: tiered pricing with implicit guarantee. ✓
- CTA: book Calendly. ✓
- Follow-up plan: existing 4-touch + LinkedIn DM at T2. ✓
- Success criteria: 1-2% Calendly book rate floor; pilot offer when reply rate <0.5%. ✓
- Anti-patterns refused: AI-generated quick-wins (use real audit data only). ✓

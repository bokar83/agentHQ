# Intake Questions By Topic

Source: Cole-style 8-category interview framework, translated to Boubacar's
intake context (CW Signal Session, SHIELD advisory, SW pre-build, general
brand discovery). Original Cole-template absorb dated 2026-05-11 in
`docs/reviews/absorb-followups.md` (G3 row, target 2026-05-18).

Use this file in two places:

- During the live intake call as a structured prompt menu
- Post-call when reviewing the transcript to confirm we have at least one
  usable answer in each category. Gaps = follow-up email or second call.

Categories are independent. Skip any category that does not fit the client.
Five to seven good answers across the eight categories beat 30 shallow ones.

---

## 1. Tips

What the operator has learned to do well that most peers in their niche
still get wrong. Used as content gold (newsletter, LinkedIn) and as
positioning material (the "this is who we are" sentence in their site copy).

Ask:

- What is the single biggest thing you do differently than other [niche]
  operators in your area? What changed when you started doing it?
- If a competitor copied one of your daily habits this month, which one
  would cost them the most to imitate properly?
- What is the cheapest piece of advice you got that changed how you run
  the business? What did the person say, exactly?
- Where do new hires consistently get tripped up in their first 30 days?
  What did you start telling them on day one to skip that mistake?

---

## 2. Industry stats and opinions

The numbers and contrarian claims the operator is willing to put their name
on. Used in cold-outreach copy, in CW Signal Session opening framing, and
in any "we know your industry" trust signal on a SW website.

Ask:

- What is the closing-rate range you actually see for cold inbound leads
  in your niche? Where do most peers think it is?
- What metric does everyone in your industry track that you think is
  vanity? What metric should they track instead?
- What number about your business surprises every consultant you talk to?
- What does the industry-association data say versus what you actually
  see in the field?

---

## 3. Steps they took

The sequence of decisions that got them from where they started to where
they are now. Used to write case-study copy, to extract repeatable patterns
for other clients, and to ground discovery-call stories.

Ask:

- Walk me through the move that got you from $X revenue to $Y. What
  changed first, what changed second, what changed last?
- What is the order of operations you would tell a $1M-services operator
  trying to get to $3M? First quarter, second quarter, third quarter?
- What is the move that did not work and you had to back out of? What
  signaled it was failing?
- If you had to rebuild your business from zero in the same niche, what
  would you NOT redo?

---

## 4. Habits

The recurring routines the operator runs at the daily, weekly, monthly
level. Most operators cannot name these without prompting. Used to extract
operating-system insights for systems posts and for advisory-engagement
ground-truth.

Ask:

- What is the first business decision you make every Monday?
- What is the recurring meeting on your calendar that you have never once
  cancelled?
- What do you measure weekly that no one on your team knows you measure?
- What do you do every quarter that most of your peers do once a year or
  never?

---

## 5. Key lessons

The earned insights. The "wish I had known this five years ago" line.
These are the gold for thought-leadership posts and for opening sequences
in cold-outreach emails.

Ask:

- What is the lesson you keep paying for because you have not fully
  internalized it yet? Be honest.
- What did your most expensive mistake teach you? (Two questions: the
  dollar number, then the lesson.)
- What lesson would you put on a billboard for operators in your niche?
- What did your mentor or first boss teach you that you only fully
  understood after running the business yourself for three years?

---

## 6. Mistakes

The specific places where the operator burned time, money, or
relationships. Used to seed contrarian-takedown content and to ground
"common-trap" outreach hooks.

Ask:

- What hire did you make that you should not have made? What signal did
  you miss?
- What tool or vendor did you buy that you regret? What was the seller's
  promise vs the reality?
- What strategic move did you make that worked tactically but cost you
  something structural? (E.g., "the discount that built the wrong
  customer base.")
- What is one thing you stopped doing in the last 12 months that you
  should have stopped 5 years ago?

---

## 7. Decision-making

How the operator decides under uncertainty. The mental models they actually
use, not the ones they tell people about. Used for SHIELD advisory scoping
and for writing copy that signals "I think the way you think".

Ask:

- Last time you had to make a five-figure-plus decision with incomplete
  information, what did you do? Talk me through the actual reasoning.
- What is the question you ask yourself when you cannot decide between
  two paths?
- When you say no to something, what is the test it failed?
- Who do you call before a hard decision? (Name pattern matters more than
  the names: spouse, mentor, accountant, peer-CEO, no one?)

---

## 8. Trending topics

The right-now noise vs the right-now signal in the operator's industry.
Used for content cadence (what to post about this month) and for
positioning ("this is what we are NOT chasing").

Ask:

- What is everyone in your industry talking about right now that you
  think is overrated?
- What is something nobody is talking about that will matter in 12
  months? Why?
- What headline keeps showing up that you find frustrating? Why is the
  framing wrong?
- What change in your industry over the last 12 months are most peers
  underestimating?

---

## Routing after the call

After the intake call, the answers feed three downstream files:

- Tips + key lessons + mistakes -> `docs/clients/<slug>/brand.md` as
  voice + positioning source
- Industry stats + decision-making -> CW outreach copy library
  (`data/cw-stats-library.md` if applicable)
- Steps they took -> case-study draft in Content Board
- Habits -> SHIELD advisory engagement notes (operating system audit)
- Trending topics -> Content Board topic queue for the next 30 days

A complete-enough intake answers at least one question in every category
above. Less than five categories with usable answers = book a 30-minute
follow-up call before any deliverable work starts.

01What I looked at

Headline, your latest post, and your About section. The one I came back to most was your headline. It is the most visible line on the page and right now it is doing the smallest job.

Note on formatting: bolds in this audit are for your eye, to mark what I am drawing attention to. Do not copy them into LinkedIn (LinkedIn does not render rich formatting in headlines or About anyway). Plain text only when you paste.

02The headline

Your current headline
Commercial Business Manager at TruGreen

This is what LinkedIn auto-populates the moment you accept a new role. Most people leave it. It does one job: it tells the reader where you work. That is the smallest job a headline can do.

One job title. Zero buyers.

A property manager scrolling LinkedIn does not need to know you work at TruGreen. They need to know that you are the person they should reach out to when their current lawn care vendor stops returning calls. Your headline should name who you help, what you fix, and why that matters today.

03The rewrite

Replace with
I help commercial property managers stop chasing their lawn care vendor and start trusting them.

Why this version works. "Commercial property managers" tells the reader "this is for me" in three words. "Stop chasing their lawn care vendor and start trusting them" names the actual pain (vendor that disappears) and the actual outcome (a person you can rely on) without using the word "excellence" or "solutions" or any of the corporate words your current About leans on. It also matches the way you actually sell, which is by being the human who shows up.

04One thing I would not change

Your post from three weeks ago opens with a line I would not change: "I love the look of fresh mow lines but not fresh, bad fertilizer application lines." That is a specific, visual, industry-insider observation. The kind of thing only someone who has stood in a hundred properties would notice. It earns the second line.

Twenty years of knowing you, I have always thought you have a voice that should be heard online. That mow-lines hook proves you can write the line. Now write more of them.

05This week's checklist

Swap your headline for the rewrite above. ~5 min.
Pin the mow-lines post to the top of your profile. ~1 min.
Write one more post this week in the same voice as the mow-lines line. Something only someone who has been on a hundred properties would notice. ~10 min.

06The recap

Headline: swap the auto-default for the buyer-first line. Post: pin the mow-lines one. Next: one new post this week, same voice. Three moves. None of them more than 10 minutes.

02The headline

Your current headline
Exec Coach | Author | Former HR Leader at DoorDash, Neighbor, LinkedIn

The pipes structure is the default most ex-corporate operators reach for when they leave a name-brand role. It tells the reader where you have been. It does not tell a CEO in chaos why they should reach out to you, today, instead of another coach with a similar pedigree.

Three titles. Zero buyers.

The headline reads quieter than the rest of your profile, which surprised me given how clearly the About, the books, and the way you write about your own training all point at the same buyer.

Two rewrite options

Option A My pick
I coach high-growth startup CEOs to lead through chaos with their integrity, their teams, and their families intact.
Option B
I help startup CEOs build the leadership operating habits that survive scale, not just the next fundraise.
Why A. I read your About first this time, and the headline is the only line on the page that does not yet sound like you. The work-and-life integration is right there: in the About, in the books, in the way you write about your kids and your training. Pulling that thread up into the headline is what I would do. B is sharper on the consulting-buyer side, but it moves you away from your real differentiator.

Why A works mechanically. Buyer-first earns the click in four words. "High-growth startup CEO" tells the reader "this is for me" before they finish the line. Then the second clause makes them care about the answer. The bigger reason A wins: it matches the voice you already use everywhere else. The headline is the only surface that has not caught up yet.

03The deeper issue, your About CTA

Your About is nine specific "here is what I have helped clients with" bullets. That is content most coaches would kill for. The problem is what happens after the last bullet: nothing. A CEO who reads all nine and gets convinced has no path forward. No DM trigger. No specific word to reply with. No reason to act today instead of bookmarking and forgetting.

Four bullets on alignment. Three on execution. Two on relational. Nine bullets, zero next steps.

Your nine bullets cluster cleanly into three buyer-pain categories. Alignment (operating manual, leadership team dynamics, vision and values, expectations and delegation). Execution (habits, productivity, calendar audit). Relational (spouse and partner and kids, accountability). Once your DM trigger lands inbound, the next pass on your About should lead with your dominant cluster. Looks like alignment to me, but you know your clients better than I do.

For today, add one sentence at the very end of your About. Something like:

"If you are leading a high-growth startup and one of these resonated, send me 'OPERATOR' on DM and I will reply within 24 hours."

Specific reply trigger. Specific buyer. Specific promise. The bullets earn the read; this line unlocks the inbound. You will know within two weeks whether the trigger word lands, and you can swap it (OPERATOR, SCALE, CHAOS all work) without rewriting the rest.

I am reading this as the operator I am, which is why I am pulling on the conversion thread. Another coach would tell you to tell more stories. Your stories work. It is the conversion path that is missing.

2What Is Costing You Bookings

The booking button does nothing

The "Book via WhatsApp" button on your site is broken because the WhatsApp number was never added. Any guest who clicks it sees an error alert. Every click is a lost booking.

Verify in 30 seconds: Open your site, click "Book via WhatsApp" or "Book a Room." You will see a browser alert that says "WhatsApp number not set yet." This is what every potential guest sees.

Your hero photo is food, not your hotel

The large image behind your hotel name on the homepage is a stock photo of African food from Unsplash. It is not your hotel. Guests who land on the page cannot see what they are booking. Your 21 actual room photos are buried in a gallery carousel that only shows 10 of them.

Verify in 30 seconds: Open your site. The large background image on the first screen is not your hotel property.

No room details means no booking intent

There is no section showing individual rooms with descriptions, types, or pricing. A guest cannot see what a Standard room offers vs. a Suite. Without that information, they cannot decide to book. They leave and book somewhere else.

Verify in 30 seconds: Scroll through your site. There is no page, section, or card listing Room 204 (Suite), Room 201 (Mini Suite), or any other room individually.

Your site is invisible when shared

When someone shares your website link on WhatsApp, Facebook, or iMessage, it shows a blank card with no image, no title, and no description. This is because Open Graph tags are missing from the site code. Every share is a missed impression.

Verify in 60 seconds: Send your website link to yourself on WhatsApp. You will see a blank or generic preview instead of your hotel photo and name.

Google cannot find you in local search

When someone searches "hotel kipe conakry" or "boutique hotel conakry guinea," your site does not appear. There is no structured data telling Google what type of business you are, where you are located, or what your phone number is. Your address is not visible anywhere on the page.

Verify in 60 seconds: Search "hotel club de kipe conakry" on Google. Your site does not appear in the local results or map pack.

3What Your Competitors Are Doing That You Are Not

Booking.com and TripAdvisor own your search results

When a traveler searches for hotels in Conakry, the first results are Booking.com and TripAdvisor listing pages for Conakry hotels. These sites rank because they have reviews, photos, room details, and structured data. Hotels that appear on these platforms get bookings from travelers who will never find a direct hotel website. Being listed is free and takes hours to set up.

Other hotels show rooms with photos and prices

Any hotel listed on Booking.com shows individual room categories with photos, bed type, capacity, and a nightly rate. Guests make decisions based on this. Without room-level information, Hotel Club de Kipe loses every comparison to any property that does provide it.

The boutique hotel market in Conakry is wide open

No hotel in Conakry currently owns the "boutique hotel" positioning in search or social. No property has structured content targeting French-speaking travelers from Guinea, Senegal, or Mali. This is a first-mover opportunity that exists right now.

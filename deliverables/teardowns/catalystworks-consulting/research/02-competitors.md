# Phase 3: Competitive Niche Scrape

**Target:** Catalyst Works (https://catalystworks.consulting/)
**Scraped:** 2026-05-01
**Niche queries:** "AI consulting Utah" + "fractional AI advisor" + "AI strategy facilitator" + "AI transformation consulting"
**Scraped competitors:** 5 (live DOM via Playwright; Firecrawl MCP not connected, fallback used per skill rule)

---

## Verdict at a glance

Catalyst Works has **one nearly-identical competitor** (SMB Strategy Consultants, same diagnosis-before-prescription frame, same constraint vocabulary, slightly different brand) and four positioned-elsewhere players (broad AI implementation shops + one enterprise gen AI builder). The market gap Catalyst Works owns: **paid first-call ($497) with a written 1-page deliverable in 24 hours**. No competitor in this batch sells a productized 90-min diagnostic with a fixed price above the fold. They all sell a free consult that ends in a custom-quote proposal.

**Translation:** Catalyst Works' offer architecture is more advanced than the segment. Visual craft and SEO/local presence trail the segment. **Polish what's already differentiated; don't reinvent.**

**Positioning vector:** identical to Catalyst Works.

| Attribute | Their pitch |
|---|---|
| H1 | "You want AI to give your business leverage... but it's not that simple. We find out why AI isn't being adopted and show you the right implementation path." |
| Key H2 | "Diagnosis before Prescription" |
| Frame | "We are the firm owners hire after AI has already failed." |
| ICP | "Owner-led service firms" |
| Method | Identify the real constraint (workflow, owner dependency, team readiness, role clarity, economics) |
| Offer | "AI Implementation Strategy Call" (price not shown above fold) |
| CTA | "BOOK THE AI IMPLEMENTATION STRATEGY CALL" (consistent across page) |
| Visual | Light theme, Plus Jakarta Sans, white background, pro photography |

**Overlap with Catalyst Works:**
- Same audience: owner-led SMBs
- Same diagnostic-before-prescription frame
- Same constraint vocabulary
- Same "the real problem isn't the tools" thesis
- Same single-CTA discipline

**What they do better:**
1. Light, premium visual treatment with photography (Catalyst Works is text-only on dark)
2. Single H1 (Catalyst Works has 1 H1 but rendering is broken on mobile sticky)
3. Multiple section H2s frame distinct objections (Catalyst Works covers same ground but in fewer sections)

**Where Catalyst Works wins:**
1. Stated price above the fold ($497 vs theirs hidden behind a form)
2. Stated time-to-deliverable (24 hours, 1 page, written) vs theirs vague
3. Scarcity tag ("3 slots this month") creates immediate FOMO
4. Distinctive italic-clay accent on emphasis words; their type is conventional
5. Stronger founder bio depth (20+ years, 4 continents, GE) is on-site

**Market gap:** SMB Strategy Consultants is the closest thing to Catalyst Works' twin. Differentiator must be sharper. **Add a productized lead-magnet score (the Friction Audit already does this) and a public price the moment a prospect lands.** Catalyst Works already has both. Visual upgrade closes the perceived gap.

## The 3 takeaways for the redesign brief

### Takeaway 1: Catalyst Works' offer architecture is ALREADY ahead of the segment.

The $497 productized Signal Session with a 1-page written deliverable in 24 hours is **the differentiator no one else has**. The redesign must double down on this, not bury it. Keep it above the fold. Make the price prominent. Make the deliverable specific.

### Takeaway 2: The closest competitor (SMB Strategy Consultants) wins on visual craft, not strategy.

Same frame, same audience, same vocabulary, but they have light-theme premium photography and a polished hero. Catalyst Works wins on offer specificity and scarcity but loses on first-impression craft. **Visual polish is the gap.** Specifically: real logo in header, hero photograph or single signature image (founder portrait if no other option), em-dash scrub, focus indicator fix, contrast fix.

### Takeaway 3: There is whitespace in "diagnosis-first AI strategy with founder credibility."

Opinosis owns "deep technical AI consultancy." Fractional AI owns "enterprise gen AI engineering." SMB Strategy Consultants owns "diagnosis-before-prescription strategy." Catalyst Works can sharpen its corner of the market by leaning harder on **Boubacar's biography** (20 years across 4 continents, GE leadership, $500K executive program) as a credibility moat that no competitor has. Move the founder photo and bio higher in the page. Treat it as a hero asset.

## Recommended visual direction for the proposed mockup

Pull from these competitor signals:
1. **Fractional AI**, dark theme craft level, image richness without busy-ness
2. **SMB Strategy Consultants**, clean editorial layout, generous whitespace at section boundaries
3. **Opinosis**, explicit geographic SEO marker (subtle: a "Serving SMBs in Atlanta, Miami, Dubai..." line is already in the footer, surface it higher)

Anti-patterns to avoid (what 4 of 5 do badly):
- Generic agency template hero
- 6 service path cards on the homepage
- "Contact us for a quote" (Catalyst Works already avoids this)
- Stock photography of robots / circuit boards / glowing brains

## Anti-patterns to avoid (sourced from BOTH design audit + competitor scrape)

From design audit (`01b-design-audit.md`):
- Don't keep the text-logotype where the real CW mark belongs
- Don't keep em-dashes in body copy
- Don't keep broken nav anchors

From competitor scrape (this doc):
- Don't bury the $497 price (4 of 5 competitors hide pricing, Catalyst Works' transparency is a moat)
- Don't add 6 service tiles on the homepage (Salt Lake City AI does this; it dilutes the single CTA)
- Don't pivot to "we build AI software" (Advisor Labs / Fractional AI own that, different segment)
- Don't go light-theme (it would erase the Fractional AI-tier visual identity Catalyst Works is positioned for)
